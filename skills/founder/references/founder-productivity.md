# Founder Productivity

Deep work, time architecture, and sustainable high performance for founders managing multiple priorities. Evidence-based frameworks from Cal Newport, Tony Schwartz, and field research on CEO time management.

## The Productivity Equation

**High-Quality Work = Time Spent × Intensity of Focus** (Cal Newport)

A 40-hour time-blocked week produces the same output as a 60+ hour unstructured week. The goal is not more hours — it's more focus per hour.

## Context Switching — The Silent Killer

- Takes **23 minutes 15 seconds** to fully regain focus after an interruption
- Knowledge workers toggle between apps **1,200+ times per day**
- Interruptions as short as **5 seconds triple error rates** in complex work
- Cost: ~4 hours of productive time lost per week

**For multi-venture founders, batching ruthlessly is non-negotiable.**

## Time Architecture Systems

### Day Theming (Jack Dorsey model)

When running multiple ventures/responsibilities:
- Monday: Management and operations
- Tuesday: Product and engineering
- Wednesday: Marketing and growth
- Thursday: Partnerships and BD
- Friday: Culture, hiring, strategy

**Why it works:** Creates "profound cognitive alignment impossible through rapid context switching" — each day builds momentum in one domain.

### 90-Minute Focus Blocks (Tony Schwartz)

Aligned with ultradian rhythms — your body's natural 90-minute energy cycles:
1. **90 minutes** of deep, focused work
2. **15-20 minutes** recovery break (walk, stretch, NOT phone)
3. Repeat 3-4 times per day
4. Peak hours (usually morning) for hardest cognitive work

### Time Blocking (Cal Newport)

Every minute of your day is assigned to a block:
- **Deep work blocks** (2-4 hours): No email, no Slack, no meetings. Phone off.
- **Shallow work blocks** (1-2 hours): Email, admin, quick decisions
- **Meeting blocks**: Batched together, never scattered through the day
- **Buffer blocks** (30 min): Between major blocks for overflow

## The Daily Routine

### Morning (protect fiercely)
1. Wake at consistent time regardless of sleep
2. Make bed (first win of the day)
3. Hydrate before coffee
4. Exercise 20-30 minutes
5. **No phone/email for first 60 minutes** — this is for planning and deep work
6. Identify 1-3 MITs (Most Important Tasks)
7. Start with hardest cognitive task during peak energy

### Midday
- Batch email and comms into 2-3 designated windows
- Meetings clustered together (not scattered)
- Lunch away from desk (recovery, not multitasking)

### Evening
- End work by 5:30-6:00 PM with **shutdown ritual**:
  - Review today's progress
  - Write tomorrow's 1-3 MITs
  - Close all work tabs
  - Say "shutdown complete" (literally — Cal Newport's ritual cue)
- **Disconnect completely** — evening recovery is non-negotiable

## Weekly Planning Session

**When:** Sunday evening or Friday afternoon, 60 minutes

**Process:**
1. Review the previous week: wins, learnings, misses
2. Check progress against quarterly objectives
3. Set the week's 3-5 key priorities
4. Time-block calendar for deep work sessions
5. Schedule exercise and recovery
6. Ask: "If this week goes perfectly, what will I have accomplished?"

## Decision Fatigue & Decision Journal

Decision fatigue is real — cognitive load deteriorates judgment over the day. Use these systems to protect decision quality:

For each major decision, record:
- Date and decision description
- Mental/physical state when deciding
- Alternatives considered
- Expected outcome
- Confidence level (1-10)
- Review date

Return after outcomes are known. Judge decisions by process, not results. This combats hindsight bias.

## Warren Buffett's 5/25 Rule

1. Write down your top 25 goals
2. Circle your top 5
3. The remaining 20 = your "avoid at all costs" list

Items 6-25 are dangerous BECAUSE you care about them. They're appealing distractions from what matters most.

## Energy Management > Time Management

**Tony Schwartz's 4 energy dimensions:**

| Dimension | Fuel | Drain |
|-----------|------|-------|
| **Physical** | Sleep, exercise, nutrition | Sleep debt, sedentary, junk food |
| **Emotional** | Positive relationships, gratitude | Conflict, isolation, negativity |
| **Mental** | Focus, prioritization | Multitasking, interruptions |
| **Spiritual** | Purpose, values alignment | Meaningless work, values conflict |

**Manage energy, not just time.** Schedule recovery as seriously as work.

## Productivity Anti-Patterns

- **Busy ≠ productive** — If you can't name today's #1 priority, you're busy, not productive
- **"Always on" culture** — Slack/email at midnight is not dedication, it's poor boundaries
- **Meeting bloat** — Every meeting needs: agenda, time limit, clear outcome, "could this be async?"
- **Productivity porn** — Spending more time optimizing your system than doing the work
- **Hero mode** — Working 80+ hours is not sustainable. Research shows cognitive performance drops sharply after 50 hours/week
