# Book Insights Index

Quick-reference: 3-5 key takeaways per book from the founder-ai-mentor knowledge base (10 books, 2,019 chunks). Organized by topic for fast lookup.

## Sales & Revenue

### Never Split the Difference — Chris Voss (227 chunks)
**Topic:** Negotiation tactics from a former FBI hostage negotiator

1. **Tactical empathy** — Understand the other side's feelings and mindset, then vocalize it. "It seems like you're worried about..."
2. **Mirroring** — Repeat the last 1-3 words they said. Creates rapport and gets them to elaborate without asking questions.
3. **Labeling** — Name their emotion: "It sounds like..." / "It seems like..." Diffuses negative emotions, reinforces positive ones.
4. **Calibrated questions** — "How am I supposed to do that?" / "What about this is important to you?" Gives them the illusion of control while you steer.
5. **The Ackerman model** — Negotiation price anchoring: offer 65% → 85% → 95% → 100% of target. Use precise numbers (not round) at each step.

### $100M Offers — Alex Hormozi (163 chunks)
**Topic:** Creating offers so good people feel stupid saying no

1. **Value equation:** Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood / Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice. Maximize top, minimize bottom.
2. **Grand Slam Offer** — Identify every obstacle between customer and dream outcome, then create solutions for each.
3. **Price to value, not to cost** — Charge based on the value you create, not what it costs you to deliver.
4. **Guarantee stacking** — Unconditional, conditional, anti-guarantees. Remove risk from the buyer.
5. **Naming and framing** — The name of your offer matters. Use: result + timeframe + container word. "6-Week Profit Accelerator" > "Consulting Package."

### $100M Leads — Alex Hormozi (281 chunks)
**Topic:** How to get strangers to want to buy your stuff

1. **4 core lead gen methods:** Warm outreach, cold outreach, content (free), paid ads. Master one before adding another.
2. **Warm outreach first** — Start with people who already know you. It's free, fast, and high conversion.
3. **Cold outreach at scale** — Personalized volume. 100 cold contacts/day = pipeline in 30 days.
4. **Content as lead gen** — Give away your best stuff for free. Build audience → trust → demand.
5. **Lead magnets** — Solve a narrow problem completely for free. Makes the prospect think "if the free stuff is this good..."

## Strategy & Thinking

### Zero to One — Peter Thiel (114 chunks)
**Topic:** How to build companies that create new things (0→1, not 1→n)

1. **Competition is for losers** — Don't compete. Build a monopoly by being 10x better at something specific.
2. **The power law** — One investment/decision/product will return more than all others combined. Focus accordingly.
3. **Secrets** — Every great company is built on a secret: something true that nobody else agrees with. Ask: "What important truth do very few people agree with you on?"
4. **Start small and monopolize** — Dominate a tiny market first, then expand. Amazon started with books, not "everything."
5. **Definite optimism** — Plan the future you want. Don't just react to what happens.

## Product & Growth

### Hooked — Nir Eyal (129 chunks)
**Topic:** How to build habit-forming products

1. **The Hook Model:** Trigger → Action → Variable Reward → Investment. Cycle creates habit.
2. **External triggers** (emails, notifications) start the cycle. **Internal triggers** (emotions: boredom, loneliness, uncertainty) sustain it.
3. **Variable rewards** — Three types: Tribe (social), Hunt (resources/info), Self (mastery/completion). Variability is the key to engagement.
4. **Investment** — User puts something in (data, content, followers, reputation) that makes the product better next time. Increases switching cost.
5. **Manipulation matrix** — Ethical check: Does your product materially improve the user's life? Would you use it yourself?

### Traction — Gabriel Weinberg & Justin Mares (163 chunks)
**Topic:** How to get traction (customers) for your startup

1. **Bullseye Framework** — Test 3 channels in inner ring, move outward. Don't spread thin across all 19.
2. **19 traction channels** — viral marketing, PR, unconventional PR, SEM, social ads, offline ads, SEO, content marketing, email marketing, engineering as marketing, targeting blogs, BD, sales, affiliate, existing platforms, trade shows, offline events, speaking, community building.
3. **50% rule** — Spend 50% of time on product, 50% on traction. Most founders spend 100% on product and wonder why no one comes.
4. **Traction trumps everything** — With traction, fundraising/hiring/press are easy. Without it, everything is hard.
5. **Test cheaply, scale what works** — Spend $500 and a week testing each channel. Double down on the winner.

## Positioning & Market

### Obviously Awesome — April Dunford (77 chunks)
**Topic:** How to position your product so customers get it immediately

1. **5-step positioning process:** Competitive alternatives → Unique attributes → Value (for customers) → Best-fit customer → Market category.
2. **Start with competitive alternatives** — What would customers do if you didn't exist? (Often "do nothing" or "use spreadsheet.")
3. **Positioning is context** — Same product positioned differently attracts completely different customers.
4. **Best-fit customers first** — Don't position for everyone. Position for the customers who love you most, then expand.
5. **Market category choice** — Existing category (easy to understand) vs new category (harder but own it). Choose based on how much education is needed.

## Methodology & Process

### The Lean Startup — Eric Ries (185 chunks)
**Topic:** Build-Measure-Learn, validated learning, systematic innovation

1. **Build-Measure-Learn loop** — Plan backwards: What to learn? → What to measure? → Minimum to build?
2. **Validated learning** — Not "we think" but "we tested and know." Behavior > words.
3. **MVP = minimum experiment** — The lightest thing that tests the riskiest assumption.
4. **Innovation accounting** — Track actionable metrics (retention, activation) not vanity (signups, downloads).
5. **Pivot or persevere** — Structured decision after each experiment cycle. If metrics plateau despite effort, pivot.

### The Startup Owner's Manual — Steve Blank (267 chunks)
**Topic:** Step-by-step customer development process

1. **Customer Discovery → Validation → Creation → Building.** Never skip steps.
2. **Earlyvangelists** — Find customers who have the problem, know they have it, have budget, and have already tried to solve it.
3. **Market type determines everything** — Existing, re-segmented, or new market? Each needs completely different GTM.
4. **Get out of the building** — No business plan survives first contact with customers. Founders must do the interviews.
5. **Premature scaling kills startups** — The #1 cause of startup death is scaling before achieving product-market fit.
