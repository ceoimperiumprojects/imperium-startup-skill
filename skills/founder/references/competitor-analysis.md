# Competitor Analysis

## Research Framework

### 1. Identify Competitors
- **Direct:** Same solution, same problem
- **Indirect:** Different solution, same problem
- **Potential future:** Could enter the space
- **Status quo:** Doing nothing / manual workaround (often your biggest competitor)

### 2. Research Each Competitor

| Dimension | How to Research |
|-----------|----------------|
| Product | Try the product. Screenshots. Feature list. |
| Customers | G2/Capterra reviews. Case studies. Social proof on their site. |
| Pricing | Pricing page. Transparent? Per-seat? Usage? |
| Traction | Crunchbase (funding). LinkedIn (team size). SimilarWeb (traffic). |
| Positioning | Homepage headline. How they describe themselves. |
| Weaknesses | Negative reviews. Social media complaints. Missing features. |

### 3. Build 2x2 Positioning Map

Choose axes where your product is in the top-right:
- Ease of use ↔ Power
- Price ↔ Value delivered
- Self-serve ↔ Enterprise
- Speed to value ↔ Depth of solution
- Specialized ↔ General purpose

### 4. Identify Opportunities
- **White space:** Segments or needs nobody serves well
- **Underserved:** Groups poorly served by existing solutions
- **Overserved:** Groups paying for features they don't need
- **Non-consumption:** People who should use a solution but don't

## Competitive Monitoring

### What to Track
- Pricing changes
- New feature releases
- Funding announcements
- Key hires
- Marketing messaging changes
- Customer reviews (new positive/negative)
- Traffic trends (SimilarWeb monthly)

### How Often
- Monthly: Quick scan of competitor homepages and social
- Quarterly: Full competitive review with team
- Ad hoc: When major announcements happen

## Competitive Strategy

### Don't
- Copy competitor features blindly
- Compete on features alone (race to the bottom)
- Ignore competitors entirely (overconfidence)
- Obsess over competitors (takes focus from customers)

### Do
- Understand why customers choose them (and you)
- Find angles they can't easily replicate
- Talk to their churned customers
- Compete on a dimension they can't match
