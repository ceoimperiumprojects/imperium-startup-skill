# Idea Validation

## The Five Fatal Questions

### Q1: Who is the customer, and would they pay today?
- Name the specific person (job title, company stage, situation)
- Estimate willingness to pay and justify it
- If "maybe" — that's a no

### Q2: Why hasn't someone built this already?
- List who has tried (there's always someone)
- Why they failed or succeeded
- What's different about NOW? (timing, tech, regulation, behavior)

### Q3: What's the distribution advantage?
- How will the first 100 users find this?
- Built-in growth loop, or paid/manual acquisition?
- If the only answer is "content and SEO" — red flag for a startup

### Q4: Can this be a big business, or is it a feature?
- Standalone product, or incumbent's next feature update?
- Natural revenue ceiling
- What needs to be true for $1M ARR?

### Q5: Can the founder actually build this?
- Required skills and resources
- Hardest technical or operational challenge
- "First hire" dependency?

## Validation Experiments

### Types
1. **Landing page test:** Describe value prop, measure signups (1 day, $0-100)
2. **Concierge MVP:** Deliver the service manually (1-2 weeks, $0)
3. **Wizard of Oz:** Looks automated, actually manual (2-4 weeks, low cost)
4. **Pre-sales:** Sell before building — collect LOIs, deposits (1-2 weeks, $0)
5. **Smoke test ads:** Run ads to landing page, measure CTR + signups (3-5 days, $50-200)
6. **Fake door test:** Add a button for a feature that doesn't exist, measure clicks (1 day, $0)

### Experiment Design Template
- **Hypothesis:** "We believe [target user] will [action] because [reason]"
- **Test:** How we'll test it (exact steps)
- **Metric:** What we'll measure
- **Success criteria:** Specific number that means "validated"
- **Time:** Maximum duration
- **Cost:** Maximum spend

## Market Sizing (Bottom-Up)

### Formula
1. **TAM:** Total # of potential users × average revenue per user
2. **SAM:** Subset you can realistically serve (geography, segment, capability)
3. **SOM:** What you can capture in Year 1 (be brutally conservative)

### Bottom-Up Method (Preferred)
- Start with # of companies/people in your ICP
- Narrow by geography, size, industry
- Multiply by realistic ARPU
- Apply realistic conversion rates

### Top-Down Method (Sanity Check Only)
- Industry market size reports
- "If we get X% of a $Y market..."
- Use ONLY as a sanity check — never as primary sizing
