# Persona Generation

## Proto-Persona Template

### Structure
- **Name:** Descriptive (e.g., "Startup Sarah" or "Enterprise Ed")
- **Photo:** Stock photo (makes it feel real)
- **Bio:** 2-3 sentences — role, company, situation
- **Quote:** What they'd say about their problem ("I spend 4 hours every week just...")
- **Goals:** Top 2-3 things they want to achieve
- **Pains:** Top 2-3 frustrations
- **Behaviors:** How they currently cope
- **Decision factors:** What matters when choosing a solution
- **Channels:** Where they spend time (LinkedIn, Reddit, conferences)
- **Budget:** Who controls it, how much, buying process

### Rules
- Based on evidence (interviews, data), NOT imagination
- Specific > generic ("VP of Sales at 50-person B2B SaaS" NOT "business professional")
- Maximum 3 personas to start — focus beats coverage
- Update as you learn more (living document)
- Include anti-personas (who this is NOT for)

### Anti-Persona
Define who you're NOT building for:
- "Enterprise companies with >500 employees" (too complex for our product)
- "Students or hobbyists" (won't pay enough)
- "Technical users who want to build custom solutions" (self-serve mismatch)

## Persona Validation

### How to Know Personas Are Wrong
- Marketing copy based on personas doesn't resonate
- Sales conversations don't match persona descriptions
- Product features for personas aren't getting used
- Customer success finds different patterns than personas predict

### How to Fix
- Talk to 5-10 more customers
- Analyze actual customer data (who's using, who's paying, who's churning)
- Look for segments you missed
- Revise and re-test
