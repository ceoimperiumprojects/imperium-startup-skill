# User Interviews Guide

## Interview Structure (20-30 min)

### 1. Context (3 min)
- "Tell me about your role and what a typical week looks like"
- "How do you currently handle [problem area]?"
- Build rapport, understand their world

### 2. Problem Exploration (12 min)
- "What's the hardest part about [problem]?"
- "When's the last time that happened? Walk me through it."
- "What have you tried to solve it?"
- "What don't you love about those solutions?"
- "How often does this come up?"
- "How much does it cost you (time/money/stress)?"

### 3. Solution Reaction (5 min, optional)
- Only if you have something to show
- Show, don't pitch
- Observe reactions and body language
- Ask: "What's your first reaction?"
- Ask: "What would make you not use this?"

### 4. Wrap-up (5 min)
- "Who else should I talk to about this?"
- "Can I follow up with you when we have more to show?"
- "Anything else I should know?"

## Signal Detection

### Green Flags (Trust These)
| Signal | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Specific past examples | "Last Tuesday, I spent 3 hours on..." |
| Emotion about the problem | Gets visibly frustrated or excited |
| Current spending | "We pay $500/month for a tool that barely works" |
| Active search | "I've been looking for something like this" |
| Commitment | Offers time, money, intro, or reputation |

### Red Flags (Ignore These)
| Signal | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Compliments | "That's a great idea!" |
| Future tense | "I would definitely use that" |
| Generic claims | "Everyone has this problem" |
| Feature requests | "You should also add..." |
| Polite agreement | "Sure, that sounds useful" |

## Synthesis Process

### After Each Interview (5 min)
1. Write key facts (not opinions) immediately
2. Note surprising or unexpected insights
3. Flag assumptions that were confirmed or invalidated
4. Rate problem severity (1-5)

### After 5-10 Interviews
1. Cluster common themes
2. Identify patterns (what do 3+ people say?)
3. Map the customer journey based on their stories
4. Identify the "hair on fire" problems
5. Decide: Is the problem worth solving?

## Recruiting Interviewees

### Where to Find Them
- Your existing network
- LinkedIn outreach (explain the purpose, not your product)
- Twitter/X engagement
- Community forums (Reddit, Slack groups)
- Conferences and meetups
- Referrals from previous interviewees

### Outreach Template
```
Hi [Name],

I'm researching how [role]s handle [problem area]. I'm talking to people like you to understand the challenges better.

Would you be open to a 20-minute conversation? No pitch — just trying to learn.

Happy to share what I learn from the research as a thank you.
```
