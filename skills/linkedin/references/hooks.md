# LinkedIn Hooks Library — 55+ Proven Hooks

Complete library of scroll-stopping hooks organized by category. Each hook includes the formula template AND a filled example. Built from analysis of posts generating 121K+ impressions.

## The Rules of Great Hooks

- Under 20 words (ideally under 12)
- Must work BEFORE the "see more" fold (first 140-210 characters)
- Creates a curiosity gap — reader MUST click to resolve the tension
- Specific > vague ("$47K" beats "a lot of money")
- Emotional charge — surprise, disagreement, recognition, aspiration
- Never start with "I'm excited..." / "I'm humbled..." / "Big news!"
- Posts starting with "You" or a number get 23% more engagement than posts starting with "I"

## Category 1: Curiosity Hooks

Create an information gap the reader can only close by reading more. The brain treats an open question like an itch that needs scratching.

### 1.1
**Template:** "I spent [time/money] doing [X]. Here's what nobody talks about."
**Example:** "I spent 3 months cold-calling 500 CEOs. Here's what nobody talks about."

### 1.2
**Template:** "I [did unexpected thing]. The result changed everything."
**Example:** "I deleted our entire marketing funnel. Revenue went up 22%."

### 1.3
**Template:** "There's a reason [successful people] never [common action]."
**Example:** "There's a reason top-performing sales reps never send proposals first."

### 1.4
**Template:** "[Something surprising happened] and it taught me [topic]."
**Example:** "A customer yelled at me on a Zoom call. It was the best feedback I ever received."

### 1.5
**Template:** "I was wrong about [thing everyone does] for [time period]."
**Example:** "I was wrong about networking for 10 years. Here's what I finally understood."

### 1.6
**Template:** "The real reason [outcome] has nothing to do with [expected cause]."
**Example:** "The real reason your landing page doesn't convert has nothing to do with the copy."

### 1.7
**Template:** "I discovered [something] by accident. It's now our [biggest thing]."
**Example:** "I discovered our best sales channel by accident. It's now 60% of our pipeline."

### 1.8
**Template:** "[Expected positive experience]. [Reality was completely different]."
**Example:** "I hired the candidate with the perfect resume. They lasted 3 weeks."

## Category 2: Contrarian Hooks

Challenge what everyone believes. These generate the most comments because people feel compelled to agree OR disagree.

### 2.1
**Template:** "Unpopular opinion: [bold statement]."
**Example:** "Unpopular opinion: Networking events are a waste of time for introverts."

### 2.2
**Template:** "Stop [common advice everyone gives]."
**Example:** "Stop telling junior developers to 'just Google it.'"

### 2.3
**Template:** "[Common belief] is a lie. Here's proof."
**Example:** "'Follow your passion' is a lie. Here's proof."

### 2.4
**Template:** "Everyone is [doing X]. Almost everyone is wrong."
**Example:** "Everyone is obsessing over AI. Almost everyone is using it wrong."

### 2.5
**Template:** "'[Common advice]' — worst advice I've ever heard."
**Example:** "'Post every day on LinkedIn' — worst advice I've ever heard."

### 2.6
**Template:** "I stopped [popular thing] and [positive result happened]."
**Example:** "I stopped setting goals and my company grew 3x."

### 2.7
**Template:** "[Respected practice] is killing your [important metric]."
**Example:** "Your weekly team meeting is killing your team's productivity."

### 2.8
**Template:** "The [industry] doesn't have a [obvious problem] problem. It has a [real problem] problem."
**Example:** "Startups don't have a funding problem. They have a focus problem."

## Category 3: Story Hooks

Drop the reader into a moment. Make them feel like they're there. Vivid, specific, sensory.

### 3.1
**Template:** "[Time period] ago I was [humble situation]. Today I [impressive outcome]."
**Example:** "2 years ago I was sleeping on my cofounder's couch. Today we have 43 employees."

### 3.2
**Template:** "Last [day/week], something happened that I can't stop thinking about."
**Example:** "Last Tuesday, something happened in our standup that I can't stop thinking about."

### 3.3
**Template:** "The moment I realized [insight], everything changed."
**Example:** "The moment I realized my best employee was about to quit, everything changed."

### 3.4
**Template:** "I almost [drastic action]. Here's what stopped me."
**Example:** "I almost shut down my company last March. Here's what stopped me."

### 3.5
**Template:** "My [unexpected person] said something that changed how I [area]."
**Example:** "My 8-year-old daughter said something that changed how I lead my team."

### 3.6
**Template:** "I got [rejected/failed] [specific number] times. It was the best thing that happened."
**Example:** "I got rejected from 47 jobs in a row. It was the best thing that ever happened to my career."

### 3.7
**Template:** "[Person] called me into [specific place] at [specific time]. I knew it was over."
**Example:** "My manager called me into a conference room at 4:47 PM on a Friday. I knew it was over."

## Category 4: Numbers Hooks

Specific numbers create instant credibility and curiosity. The more precise the number, the more believable.

### 4.1
**Template:** "[X]% of [group] fail at [thing]. Here's why."
**Example:** "83% of sales teams miss quota every quarter. Here's why."

### 4.2
**Template:** "From $[small] to $[big] in [time]. Here's exactly how."
**Example:** "From $0 to $50K MRR in 8 months. Here's exactly how."

### 4.3
**Template:** "I [analyzed/studied/reviewed] [large number] [things]. [Number] patterns emerged."
**Example:** "I reviewed 500 startup pitch decks this year. 3 patterns separated winners from losers."

### 4.4
**Template:** "[Specific metric] in [timeframe]. No [common tactic]."
**Example:** "47,000 followers in 6 months. No ads. No engagement pods. No hacks."

### 4.5
**Template:** "It took me [long time] to learn what I'm about to share in [short time]."
**Example:** "It took me 15 years to learn what I'm about to share in 2 minutes."

### 4.6
**Template:** "[Number] [things] that [outcome]. (Save this list.)"
**Example:** "11 free tools that replaced $3,200/month in SaaS subscriptions. (Save this list.)"

### 4.7
**Template:** "We [achieved thing] with only [surprisingly small resource]."
**Example:** "We hit $1M ARR with only 2 people and zero funding."

### 4.8
**Template:** "1 [small action] = [disproportionately large result]."
**Example:** "1 email changed our churn rate by 34%. Here's what it said."

## Category 5: Challenge Hooks

Directly challenge the reader's current behavior. Creates immediate tension and self-reflection.

### 5.1
**Template:** "Stop doing [common practice]. Do this instead."
**Example:** "Stop sending 'just checking in' follow-up emails. Do this instead."

### 5.2
**Template:** "If you're still [outdated practice] in [year], you're [consequence]."
**Example:** "If you're still writing 10-page proposals in 2025, you're losing deals."

### 5.3
**Template:** "You don't need [thing everyone says you need]. You need [alternative]."
**Example:** "You don't need more leads. You need better qualification."

### 5.4
**Template:** "The #1 mistake [professionals] make (and how to fix it)."
**Example:** "The #1 mistake first-time managers make (and how to fix it)."

### 5.5
**Template:** "You're doing [thing] wrong. Here's the fix."
**Example:** "You're running your 1-on-1s wrong. Here's the fix."

### 5.6
**Template:** "If [situation], you need to read this."
**Example:** "If your best performer just quit, you need to read this."

## Category 6: Authority Hooks

Leverage experience and credibility to earn attention. The credibility marker must be specific and verifiable.

### 6.1
**Template:** "After [X years/deals/users], here's what I know for sure."
**Example:** "After hiring 200+ people and firing 30, here's what I know for sure."

### 6.2
**Template:** "I've [impressive credibility marker]. The #1 lesson: [insight]."
**Example:** "I've built 4 companies from zero. The #1 lesson: hire slow, fire fast is wrong."

### 6.3
**Template:** "I've [verb] [large number] [things]. [X] [things] stood out."
**Example:** "I've interviewed 150 founders. 5 habits separated the successful ones."

### 6.4
**Template:** "As a [role] who [credibility], here's my honest take on [topic]."
**Example:** "As a CTO who's scaled teams from 3 to 80, here's my honest take on remote work."

### 6.5
**Template:** "I asked [impressive number/type of people] one question. Their answer surprised me."
**Example:** "I asked 50 VCs what they look for first in a pitch. Their answer surprised me."

## Category 7: Question Hooks

Open with a question that forces the reader to self-reflect. Best when the question reveals a tension.

### 7.1
**Template:** "Why do [professionals] keep making this mistake?"
**Example:** "Why do experienced founders keep hiring their friends?"

### 7.2
**Template:** "What would you do if [scenario]?"
**Example:** "What would you do if your cofounder wanted to pivot and you didn't?"

### 7.3
**Template:** "Have you ever [relatable situation]?"
**Example:** "Have you ever worked 80-hour weeks and felt like you accomplished nothing?"

### 7.4
**Template:** "Why does nobody talk about [elephant in the room]?"
**Example:** "Why does nobody talk about how lonely leadership actually is?"

### 7.5
**Template:** "Is [common practice] actually [negative]?"
**Example:** "Is hustle culture actually just poor planning?"

## Category 8: Confession Hooks

Vulnerability creates connection. Admitting mistakes builds trust faster than showcasing wins.

### 8.1
**Template:** "I'm going to share something I've never posted about."
**Example:** "I'm going to share something I've never posted about: I almost bankrupted my first company."

### 8.2
**Template:** "My biggest failure taught me [lesson]."
**Example:** "My biggest failure — losing a $400K client — taught me more than any win."

### 8.3
**Template:** "I used to [embarrassing belief/practice]. Here's what changed."
**Example:** "I used to think more hours = more success. Here's what changed."

### 8.4
**Template:** "Here's what I wish I knew [time period] ago."
**Example:** "Here's what I wish I knew before I became a manager."

### 8.5
**Template:** "I lied to myself about [thing] for [time]."
**Example:** "I lied to myself about work-life balance for 5 years."

### 8.6
**Template:** "I don't have [thing you're supposed to have by now]."
**Example:** "I don't have a 5-year plan. I barely have a 5-week plan. And I'm OK with that."

## Category 9: Prediction Hooks

Forward-looking statements that position you as a thought leader. Must be backed by evidence, not wishful thinking.

### 9.1
**Template:** "In [timeframe], [bold prediction]. Here's why."
**Example:** "In 2 years, 80% of customer support will be fully automated. Here's why."

### 9.2
**Template:** "[Industry/role] is about to change forever. Here are the signals."
**Example:** "The marketing industry is about to change forever. Here are the 5 signals."

### 9.3
**Template:** "The [thing] that works today won't work in [timeframe]. Here's what will."
**Example:** "The cold email strategy that works today won't work in 12 months. Here's what will."

### 9.4
**Template:** "[X] will be the most important skill in [year]. Most people aren't ready."
**Example:** "Prompt engineering will be the most important skill in 2026. Most people aren't ready."

### 9.5
**Template:** "By [year], [X]% of [thing] will be [prediction]. And it won't matter."
**Example:** "By 2027, 50% of LinkedIn content will be AI-generated. And it won't matter."

## Hook Performance Tiers

Based on analysis of posts with 121K+ total impressions:

### S-Tier (consistently 20K+ impressions)
- Story hooks with specific numbers (#3.1, #3.6, #3.7)
- Contrarian + proof (#2.3, #2.5, #2.6)
- Numbers + curiosity gap (#4.1, #4.3, #4.8)
- Transformation hooks (#3.1 — "[Time] ago I was... Today I...")

### A-Tier (consistently 10K+ impressions)
- Challenge hooks (#5.1, #5.3)
- Authority + insight (#6.1, #6.5)
- Confession hooks (#8.2, #8.3)
- Unexpected juxtaposition (#1.2, #1.8)

### B-Tier (5-10K impressions, high comment rate)
- Question hooks (#7.1, #7.4)
- Prediction hooks (#9.1, #9.2)
- Curiosity hooks (#1.1, #1.6)

### Key Insight
S-tier hooks combine TWO elements:
1. **Specificity** (numbers, names, timeframes — not vague claims)
2. **Emotional tension** (surprise, vulnerability, challenge — not neutral)

A hook that has BOTH will almost always outperform one with just one element.

## Quick Hook Selection Guide

| Your content is about... | Best hook categories |
|--------------------------|---------------------|
| A personal experience/lesson | Story (#3) or Confession (#8) |
| A strong opinion | Contrarian (#2) or Challenge (#5) |
| Data or research | Numbers (#4) or Authority (#6) |
| Teaching a skill/process | Challenge (#5) or Curiosity (#1) |
| Industry trend/prediction | Prediction (#9) or Numbers (#4) |
| A win or milestone | Story (#3) — lead with the journey, not the flex |
| Client results | Numbers (#4) or Curiosity (#1) |
| Hiring | Confession (#8) — "I don't have [X]" |
| Product launch | Curiosity (#1) — lead with the pain, not the product |

## Hook Writing Process

1. **Write 3-5 hooks for every post** — never go with the first one
2. **Read each as a stranger** — would YOU click "see more"?
3. **Pick the most specific one** — vague = invisible
4. **Check the word count** — under 20 words, ideally under 12
5. **Test the curiosity gap** — does the hook create a question the body answers?
6. **Verify the emotional charge** — is there surprise, tension, or recognition?
7. **Check for "I" as first word** — if yes, try rewriting to start with "You", a number, or an action verb
