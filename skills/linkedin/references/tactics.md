# LinkedIn Advanced Tactics

Operational playbook for consistent LinkedIn growth. Beyond what to write — how to run a content engine that compounds over time.

---

## Golden Hour Strategy

The first 60-90 minutes after posting determine ~70% of your post's total reach. This is the most important window in your entire LinkedIn workflow.

### Pre-Post Phase (15-30 minutes before publishing)

- [ ] Comment meaningfully on 10-15 posts in your feed (real thoughts, 5+ words each)
- [ ] Reply to any pending comments on your previous posts
- [ ] Visit 3-5 profiles of target connections (triggers notifications)
- [ ] Engage with content from your engagement circle (reciprocity priming)
- [ ] Have your first comment ready (link, resource, or bonus insight)

### Launch Phase (0-15 minutes after publishing)

- [ ] Publish at optimal time (Tue-Thu, 8-10 AM target audience timezone)
- [ ] Immediately post your prepared first comment (with any links)
- [ ] Monitor incoming comments — reply to EVERY single one
- [ ] Your replies count as engagement signals (each reply boosts the post)
- [ ] Keep replies substantive (add value, ask follow-up questions)

### Growth Phase (15-60 minutes after publishing)

- [ ] Continue monitoring and replying to every comment
- [ ] Engage with the profiles of people who commented (visit profile, like their content)
- [ ] If comment threads develop, keep the conversation going (thread depth = highest-value signal)
- [ ] Share the post to relevant LinkedIn groups (if applicable)
- [ ] DO NOT edit the post (editing resets the algorithm)

### After Golden Hour (60+ minutes)

- [ ] Check back every 1-2 hours for the first 4 hours
- [ ] Reply to all new comments within 2 hours maximum
- [ ] Note which comments/replies sparked the best threads (learn for next time)
- [ ] If the post is performing well, engage with even MORE posts in your feed (ride the momentum)

## Commenting Strategy

Your commenting strategy may be more important than your posting strategy for growth.

### The 20-Before-1 Rule

Before publishing your post each day, leave 20 meaningful comments on other people's posts.

**Why this works:**
1. Warms up the algorithm (LinkedIn sees you as an "active member")
2. Gets your name and face in front of new audiences
3. Triggers reciprocity (people check out your content in return)
4. Builds genuine relationships with other creators
5. Your best post ideas will come from commenting conversations

### What Makes a Great Comment

**Meaningful comments (5+ words) that add to the discussion:**
- Share your own relevant experience: "This resonates. We tried the opposite at [company] and saw [result]."
- Add a data point or counter-example: "I'd add one thing: [insight]. We learned this when [brief story]."
- Ask a thoughtful follow-up: "Interesting take. Curious — did you find that [related question]?"
- Respectfully challenge: "I partially disagree on #3. In my experience, what worked was [alternative]."
- Offer a genuinely helpful resource: "We built a template for exactly this. Happy to share if useful."

**Comments to avoid:**
- "Great post!" / "Love this!" / emoji-only (worthless, looks lazy)
- Self-promotional comments ("Check out my tool...")
- "I agree" without adding anything
- Tagging people who aren't relevant
- Copy-paste responses

### Strategic Commenting Tiers

| Tier | Who | How Many | Purpose |
|------|-----|----------|---------|
| **Tier 1: Your circle** | 10-15 creators at similar level | Every post | Mutual amplification, genuine relationships |
| **Tier 2: Aspirational** | 5-10 creators 2-3x your following | 3-5x/week | Get noticed by bigger audiences |
| **Tier 3: New voices** | 5-10 new creators/week | As discovered | Network growth, fresh connections |

### Comment Timing
- Comment within first 30 minutes of a post going live (your comment appears higher)
- The post author is most likely to reply in the first 1-2 hours
- Early comments get more visibility from the post's audience

### Daily Comment Budget
- 10 quality comments minimum, 20 optimal
- Each comment: 30-90 seconds of thought
- Total time investment: 15-30 minutes daily
- ROI: Often higher than the time spent writing your own posts

## Content Calendar Framework

### Weekly Content Rhythm

| Day | Activity |
|-----|---------|
| **Monday** | Review last week's analytics. Batch-write 3-5 posts. 20 comments |
| **Tuesday** | Publish Post #1 (8-10 AM). Golden Hour protocol. 20 comments pre-post |
| **Wednesday** | Publish Post #2 (8-10 AM). Golden Hour protocol. 20 comments |
| **Thursday** | Publish Post #3 (8-10 AM). Golden Hour protocol. Content review |
| **Friday** | Optional Post #4 (lighter content). 10-15 comments. Fill swipe file. Connect with 5-10 new people |
| **Weekend** | Rest from publishing. Capture ideas. Read industry content for inspiration |

### Content Batching Process

**Weekly content session (2-3 hours, once per week):**

1. **Idea dump (20 min)**
   - Review saved notes, conversations, customer interactions
   - Scan industry news for reaction opportunities
   - Check analytics — what topics resonated last week?
   - Write down 10-15 raw ideas (one sentence each)

2. **Idea filter (10 min)**
   - Score each: Interesting? Can I add unique perspective? Relevant to audience?
   - Select 5 ideas for the week
   - Match each to a post type

3. **Hook writing (30 min)**
   - Write 3 hooks for each of the 5 posts
   - Pick the strongest hook for each
   - Test: Would YOU click "see more"?

4. **Full draft (60-90 min)**
   - Write all 5 posts in one session
   - Don't edit while writing — get ideas down
   - Follow post structure formula for each type

5. **Edit pass (30 min, ideally next day)**
   - Cut everything that doesn't serve the core idea
   - Tighten sentences, remove filler
   - Check formatting and character count (1,000-1,500 chars)

6. **Schedule**
   - Use LinkedIn's native scheduler or Buffer/Taplio
   - Tue-Thu, 8-10 AM target timezone
   - Leave 1-2 slots open for reactive/timely content

### Content Mix Per Week

| Post Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Value/Educational | 2x/week | Build authority, drive saves |
| Story/Personal | 1x/week | Build connection, drive comments |
| Opinion/Contrarian | 1x/week | Drive debate, increase reach |
| Milestone/BTS (rotating) | 1x every 2 weeks | Show progress, build investment |

### Idea Sources

**Your own experience:**
- Customer conversations (anonymized)
- Mistakes you made and lessons learned
- Decisions you're currently wrestling with
- Behind-the-scenes of your work

**External stimulation:**
- Industry reports and data releases
- Competitor announcements
- Conference talks, podcasts (react with your take)
- Comments on your previous posts (great follow-up ideas)
- Questions people DM you

**The Content Swipe File:**
Keep a running note (phone) where you capture:
- Interesting hooks you see in your feed
- Story moments from your day
- Data points that surprise you
- Questions people ask you repeatedly
This is your creative fuel tank. Fill it constantly.

## Content Repurposing — The 3-5x Rule

One core idea can become 3-5 pieces of content across formats. Don't create new ideas — extract more value from proven ones.

### The Repurposing Cascade

| Step | Format | When | Notes |
|------|--------|------|-------|
| 1 | LinkedIn text post | Week 1 | The core idea, LinkedIn-optimized |
| 2 | LinkedIn carousel | Week 1-2 | Same idea, visual slide format (2-3x reach) |
| 3 | Twitter/X thread | Week 2 | Shorter, punchier version |
| 4 | Newsletter | Week 2-3 | Expanded with deeper analysis |
| 5 | Short-form video | Week 3-4 | 60-second verbal version (TikTok/Reels/Shorts) |

### Repurposing Rules
- **Change the format, not just the platform.** Copy-pasting the same text = lazy and penalized
- **Adapt to platform norms.** LinkedIn = professional/long-form. Twitter = punchy. TikTok = personality
- **Stagger timing.** Don't publish everywhere same day. Spread across the week
- **Best-performers only.** Only repurpose posts that performed well. Let low-performers die

### Content Lifecycle
A great idea has a 90-day lifecycle:
- **Week 1:** Original post
- **Week 2:** Carousel + thread
- **Week 3:** Newsletter deep-dive
- **Week 4-6:** Video + blog post
- **Month 3:** Revisit with new data or perspective

## Monetization Pipeline

### The 70-20-10 Content Rule

| Category | % of Content | Purpose | Examples |
|----------|-------------|---------|----------|
| **Educational** | 70% | Build trust, demonstrate expertise | How-tos, frameworks, listicles, myth-busting |
| **Problem-Solution** | 20% | Show what you can do | Case studies, client results, behind-the-scenes of your work |
| **Promotional** | 10% | Convert to revenue | Product launches, hiring, direct CTAs |

**Key insight:** The 70% educational content is what earns you permission to post the 10% promotional content. If you flip the ratio, you lose the audience.

### The Conversion Funnel

```
Post → Profile Visit → Follow → DM Conversation → Call → Customer
```

Each post is the top of a funnel. Your profile is your landing page. DMs are your discovery calls.

**Metrics that matter for monetization:**
- Profile views per post (are people curious about you?)
- Follower growth rate (is your audience expanding?)
- Inbound DM rate (are people reaching out?)
- Website clicks from profile link (are they taking action?)

## A/B Testing Hooks

The hook is the single highest-leverage element. Testing systematically accelerates your learning curve.

### Testing Methods

**Method 1: Same idea, different weeks**
- Write Topic X with Hook A → publish Tuesday
- Write Topic X with Hook B → publish following Tuesday
- Compare: impressions, engagement rate, comment quality

**Method 2: Hook polling**
- Share 3 hook options with your engagement circle before publishing
- Ask: "Which one would make you click 'see more'?"
- Go with majority pick, track results

**Method 3: Comment testing**
- Use a hook as a comment on someone else's post first
- If your comment gets 10+ likes → the core idea has resonance
- Turn the high-performing comment into a full post

### What to Test

| Variable | Test |
|----------|------|
| Opening word | "I" vs "You" vs number vs question word |
| Specificity | "My company grew" vs "We went from $0 to $47K MRR" |
| Emotional register | Inspirational vs educational vs provocative |
| Length | 1-line hook vs 2-line hook |
| Structure | Statement vs question vs story opening |

### Tracking Template

| Date | Hook | Type | Impressions | Eng Rate | Comments | Learning |
|------|------|------|-------------|----------|----------|----------|
| 3/1 | "I spent $47K..." | Curiosity | 34,200 | 4.2% | 67 | Specific $ drives curiosity |
| 3/4 | "Stop sending..." | Challenge | 12,100 | 3.1% | 43 | Challenge = fewer impressions, higher comment quality |

After 20-30 posts, your audience's patterns emerge. Lean into what works.

## Inner Circle Building

Build a group of 10-15 content creators for mutual support and amplification.

### What a Good Inner Circle Looks Like
- 5-10 creators at your level (similar following, similar niche)
- 3-5 creators slightly ahead of you (aspirational, pull you up)
- 2-3 creators in adjacent niches (cross-pollinate audiences)

### How to Build It
1. Identify creators whose content you genuinely enjoy
2. Comment consistently on their posts for 4-6 weeks (not asking for anything)
3. Send a genuine DM: "Hey, I love your content on [topic]. I'm building my presence too — would you want to share drafts or connect on content strategy?"
4. Start small: share hooks for feedback, discuss what's working
5. Let it evolve naturally into a mastermind

### Inner Circle Rules
- Only engage when content genuinely interests you
- Write real, thoughtful comments (not copy-paste)
- It's OK to skip a post if it doesn't resonate
- No pressure or obligation for every post
- Leave if it becomes performative

### What Doesn't Work
- Large pods (30+ people) with mandatory engagement — LinkedIn detects and penalizes
- Comment-for-comment with no genuine interest
- Pods in completely unrelated niches (signals look unnatural)

## AI Best Practices for LinkedIn Content

### Using AI Effectively
- **Use AI for:** Structure, outline generation, hook variations, editing suggestions
- **Don't use AI for:** Your voice, your stories, your specific experiences
- **The rule:** AI writes the skeleton. You add the flesh, blood, and soul

### Avoiding AI Detection
LinkedIn is actively building AI-generated content detection. Posts flagged as AI get -30% reach, -55% engagement.

**How to stay human:**
- Add personal details AI can't know (specific times, places, names, internal feelings)
- Include imperfections (sentence fragments, informal language, thought pauses)
- Reference your specific experience (not generic "as a founder, I...")
- Break patterns (AI is very predictable in structure — vary your format)
- Read out loud — if it sounds like a textbook, rewrite it
- Add controversial or nuanced opinions (AI hedges, humans take sides)

### The AI-Assisted Workflow
1. Brain dump your idea (voice notes, messy text, bullet points)
2. Ask AI to organize the structure and suggest hooks
3. Pick the best structure, then REWRITE in your own voice
4. Add: your specific story, real numbers, genuine emotion, imperfect language
5. Cut: any line that could have been written by anyone
6. Final check: read out loud. Does it sound like YOU or like a LinkedIn coach?

## LinkedIn DM Strategy

Posts drive awareness. DMs drive relationships and revenue.

### When to DM
- Someone leaves a thoughtful comment → thank them, start a conversation
- A new follower from your target audience → welcome, ask what they're working on
- Someone views your profile multiple times → reach out with value (not a pitch)
- A mutual connection introduces you → follow up within 24 hours

### DM Templates

**After a great comment:**
"Hey [name], thanks for that comment on my [topic] post — your point about [specific thing they said] really got me thinking. Would love to connect and continue the conversation."

**New follower:**
"Hey [name], saw you just followed — appreciate it! Took a look at your profile. [Specific observation about their work]. What are you focused on right now?"

### DM Rules
- Never pitch in the first DM (ever)
- Never send automated connection messages
- Never send a wall of text about yourself
- Never follow up with "did you see my message?" after 24 hours

### The DM Pipeline
1. Genuine comment engagement (public)
2. Thank-you DM (private)
3. Brief conversation about shared interests
4. Offer to help with something specific
5. Natural evolution into professional relationship

This takes weeks or months. There are no shortcuts to trust.

### Monthly Review Checklist
1. Top 3 posts — what topics, hooks, and formats performed best?
2. Bottom 3 posts — what didn't work? Why?
3. Follower demographics — attracting the right people?
4. Content type breakdown — which post types consistently perform?
5. Posting time analysis — are certain days/times outperforming?

### The Compound Growth Curve

LinkedIn growth is exponential, not linear:
- **Month 1-3:** Slow. Feels like shouting into void (500-2K impressions/post)
- **Month 4-6:** First breakout posts (2K-10K impressions/post)
- **Month 7-12:** Compound effect kicks in (10K-50K impressions/post)
- **Month 12+:** Established authority (50K+ on good posts)

**The key insight:** Most people quit in months 2-4. The growth curve starts to inflect at month 5-6. Consistency through the "valley of despair" is the entire game.
