# Financial Modeling for Startups

## Three-Statement Model

### Income Statement (P&L)
```
Revenue
- Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
= Gross Profit (Gross Margin %)
- Operating Expenses
  - Sales & Marketing
  - Research & Development
  - General & Administrative
= Operating Income (EBITDA)
- Depreciation & Amortization
- Interest
- Taxes
= Net Income
```

### Balance Sheet
```
Assets = Liabilities + Equity

Assets:
- Cash & equivalents
- Accounts receivable
- Prepaid expenses
- Property & equipment

Liabilities:
- Accounts payable
- Deferred revenue
- Debt

Equity:
- Common stock
- Retained earnings
- Additional paid-in capital
```

### Cash Flow Statement
```
Operating Cash Flow:
  Net income
  + Depreciation
  + Changes in working capital

Investing Cash Flow:
  - Capital expenditures
  - Acquisitions

Financing Cash Flow:
  + Equity raised
  + Debt raised
  - Debt repayments
  - Dividends

= Net Change in Cash
```

## Bottom-Up Revenue Modeling

### SaaS Revenue Model
```
Month N Revenue =
  Existing customers (Month N-1)
  × Retention rate
  × ARPU
  + New customers × ARPU
  + Expansion revenue (upsells)
```

### Key Assumptions to Document
- Customer acquisition rate (monthly new customers)
- Churn rate (monthly, by cohort if possible)
- ARPU (by tier, by segment)
- Expansion rate (% of customers who upgrade)
- Sales cycle length
- Marketing spend → Lead → Customer conversion rates

## Headcount Cost Model

### Fully Loaded Cost Per Employee
```
Base salary
+ Benefits (15-25% of salary)
+ Payroll taxes (7-10%)
+ Equipment ($2-5K one-time)
+ Software tools ($200-500/month)
+ Office/remote stipend
= Fully loaded cost
```
