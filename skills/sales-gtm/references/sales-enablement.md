# Sales Enablement

## Battlecard Template

### [Competitor Name] Battlecard

**When prospect mentions [Competitor]:**

**Acknowledge (build credibility):**
"They're good at [X]. A lot of teams start there."

**Differentiate (where you win):**
"Where we differ is [specific capability]. For example, [concrete example]."

**Prove (evidence):**
"[Customer name] switched from [Competitor] because [reason]. They saw [specific result]."

**Landmine (question that exposes weakness):**
"Ask them about [specific limitation]. Most teams discover that [problem] around month 3."

**Common Objection:**
"But [Competitor] is cheaper/more established..."
→ "True, and for [use case] they work well. But for [your use case], the ROI difference is [X] because [reason]."

## Demo Script Structure

### Pre-Demo (2 min)
"Before I show you anything, let me make sure I understand your situation correctly..."
- Recap their pain points from discovery
- Confirm priorities (what matters most?)
- Set expectations for the demo

### The Demo (10-15 min)
**Rule: Show THEIR problem, not your features.**

1. Start with their #1 pain point → Show how you solve it
2. Show their #2 pain point → Show the solution
3. Show one "wow" feature they didn't expect → Create delight
4. Skip everything else (save for follow-up)

### Post-Demo (5 min)
- Social proof: "Company similar to yours saw [result]"
- Check-in: "Does this feel like it would work for your team?"
- Next step: "What would it take to move forward?"
- Timeline: "When would you want to start?"

## Objection Handling (LAER Method)

### Common Objections

| Objection | Response Framework |
|-----------|-------------------|
| "Too expensive" | "Compared to what? What's the cost of NOT solving this?" |
| "We're using [competitor]" | "What's working? What's not? Let me show you the difference." |
| "Not the right time" | "Totally understand. When is the right time? Let's plan for that." |
| "Need to think about it" | "Of course. What specifically do you need to think through? I might be able to help." |
| "Need more features" | "Which feature is the dealbreaker? Let's see if we can solve it differently." |
| "Too complex" | "Fair concern. Let me show you how [company] got started in [X] minutes." |

### LAER Method
- **L**isten: Let them finish. Don't interrupt. Show you hear them.
- **A**cknowledge: "That's a fair concern. I hear that from a lot of teams."
- **E**xplore: "Can you tell me more about what's driving that concern?"
- **R**espond: Address with evidence, social proof, or reframing.

## Discovery Call Template (15-20 min)

1. **Rapport** (2 min): Find common ground, set agenda
2. **Situation** (3 min): "Tell me about your current process for [X]"
3. **Problem** (5 min): "What's the biggest challenge with that?"
4. **Impact** (3 min): "What does that cost you in time/money/stress?"
5. **Vision** (3 min): "If you could wave a magic wand, what would it look like?"
6. **Next steps** (2 min): "Based on what you've told me, I think we can help. Here's what I'd suggest..."
