# Offer Creation

Creating offers so good people feel stupid saying no. Distilled from Alex Hormozi's "$100M Offers" (163 chunks in knowledge base).

## The Value Equation

The foundation of every great offer:

```
              Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value = ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
              Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice
```

**To increase value:**
- Increase dream outcome (bigger promise)
- Increase perceived likelihood (proof, guarantees)
- Decrease time delay (faster results)
- Decrease effort & sacrifice (easier for them)

**Key insight:** You can charge MORE by reducing time and effort than by increasing the dream outcome. A $100K result in 30 days with no effort > a $200K result in 12 months with heavy lifting.

## Grand Slam Offer Construction

### Step 1: Identify the Dream Outcome

What does the customer want? Not your product — the RESULT.
- Not "marketing software" → "50 qualified leads per month"
- Not "fitness app" → "Lose 20 lbs in 12 weeks without giving up pizza"
- Not "legal service" → "Recover $50K+ in surplus funds you didn't know you had"

### Step 2: List All Obstacles

Every problem between the customer and their dream outcome:

**Categories of obstacles:**
1. **Starting problems** — "I don't know where to begin"
2. **Skill problems** — "I don't know how to do X"
3. **Tool problems** — "I don't have the right tools"
4. **Time problems** — "I don't have enough time"
5. **Motivation problems** — "I won't stick with it"
6. **Belief problems** — "I don't think it'll work for me"

### Step 3: Create Solutions for Each Obstacle

For every obstacle, create a vehicle that eliminates it:

| Obstacle | Solution Vehicle |
|----------|-----------------|
| Don't know where to start | Step-by-step onboarding |
| Don't know how | Templates + training |
| Don't have tools | Included in package |
| No time | Done-for-you service |
| Won't stick with it | Accountability system |
| Don't believe it'll work | Guarantee + case studies |

### Step 4: Trim and Stack

- Remove solutions with low value and high cost
- Keep solutions with high value (to customer) and low cost (to you)
- Stack them together into a comprehensive offer

## Pricing Psychology

### Price to Value, Not to Cost

**Cost-based thinking:** "It costs me $100 to deliver, so I'll charge $200" (2x markup)

**Value-based thinking:** "This saves the customer $50,000/year. I'll charge $5,000" (10x ROI for them)

**The 10x rule:** Your offer should deliver at least 10x the price in perceived value. If you charge $1,000, the customer should feel they're getting $10,000+ in value.

### Pricing Anchoring

Present the value BEFORE the price:
1. "The typical cost of solving this problem is $50K+"
2. "Our clients save an average of $30K in the first year"
3. "Your investment is $4,997"

The price seems small relative to the anchor.

### Price Presentation

- Use specific numbers ($4,997 not $5,000) — implies precision and thoughtfulness
- Break into daily cost: "$4,997 = about $14/day — less than lunch"
- Compare to the cost of NOT solving: "Every month you wait costs ~$4K in lost revenue"

## Guarantee Stacking

Guarantees remove risk from the buyer and transfer it to you (where it belongs, since you control the outcome).

### Types of Guarantees

| Type | Structure | Best For |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **Unconditional** | "Full refund, no questions asked, 30 days" | Low-ticket, high volume |
| **Conditional** | "If you do X, Y, Z and don't get results → refund" | High-ticket, commitment needed |
| **Anti-guarantee** | "All sales are final — we only work with serious people" | Ultra-premium, scarcity |
| **Performance** | "We'll work for free until you hit [metric]" | Service businesses, confidence |
| **Implied** | Social proof so strong a guarantee isn't needed | Market leaders |

### Stacking Example

"Try it risk-free for 30 days. If you implement our playbook and don't see at least 20 qualified leads, we'll refund every penny AND give you our competitor analysis template free ($2,000 value)."

## Naming Your Offer

**Formula:** [Result] + [Timeframe] + [Container Word]

**Examples:**
- "6-Week Profit Accelerator" > "Consulting Package"
- "90-Day Pipeline Builder" > "Sales Training"
- "The Revenue Rescue Sprint" > "Financial Advisory"

**Naming rules:**
- Communicate the result, not the process
- Include a timeframe (creates urgency, sets expectations)
- Use a container word (Accelerator, System, Blueprint, Sprint, Playbook)
- Avoid generic terms (Program, Service, Package)

## Scarcity & Urgency (Only If Genuine)

**Legitimate scarcity:**
- Limited capacity (you can only serve 10 clients/month)
- Cohort-based (next cohort starts March 1)
- Time-sensitive bonus (early adopter pricing until Friday)

**Never fake scarcity.** Customers smell it and trust evaporates.

## Offer Testing Framework

| Test | What It Validates | Minimum Sample |
|------|-------------------|----------------|
| Landing page | Value prop resonance | 500 visitors |
| Price page click-through | Price-value perception | 200 visitors |
| Pre-sales | Willingness to pay | 10 conversations |
| Guarantee usage | Risk perception | 50 customers |
| Upsell acceptance | Offer stack value | 30 customers |

## Common Offer Mistakes

1. **Commodity pricing** — Competing on price instead of value. Race to the bottom.
2. **Feature dumping** — Listing 47 features nobody reads. Focus on outcomes.
3. **Weak guarantee** — "Satisfaction guaranteed" means nothing. Be specific.
4. **No urgency** — "Buy whenever" = "Buy never." Create legitimate urgency.
5. **Unclear transformation** — Customer can't visualize the before/after. Paint the picture.
