# Discovery & Research

## Discovery Process (6 Phases)

### Phase 1: Problem Framing
- Define the problem clearly: I am [persona] / Trying to [goal] / But [barrier] / Because [root cause]
- Validate that this is a real problem (not assumed)
- Identify who has it (specific, not generic)

### Phase 2: Research & Data Gathering
- Quantitative: Analytics, surveys, usage data
- Qualitative: Interviews (Mom Test), observation, support tickets
- Competitive: What alternatives exist? Why do people use them?

### Phase 3: Synthesis
- Cluster findings into themes
- Identify patterns across interviews
- Map the customer journey
- Build opportunity solution tree

### Phase 4: Opportunity Identification
- Score opportunities: Importance × (Importance - Satisfaction)
- Prioritize by impact and feasibility
- Validate with additional data if needed

### Phase 5: Solution Exploration
- Brainstorm solutions for top opportunities
- Prototype cheapest/fastest to test
- Test with real users (not colleagues)

### Phase 6: Decision & Handoff
- Document findings and decisions
- Create user stories and acceptance criteria
- Align with engineering on feasibility and scope

### Opportunity Solution Tree (Teresa Torres)
```
Desired Outcome (measurable)
├── Opportunity 1 (unmet need)
│   ├── Solution A (testable)
│   └── Solution B (testable)
├── Opportunity 2
│   └── Solution C
└── Opportunity 3
    ├── Solution D
    └── Solution E
```

### Proof of Life (PoL) Probes
1. **Feasibility:** Can we build it? (Tech spike)
2. **Task-focused:** Can users do the thing? (Usability test)
3. **Narrative:** Does the story resonate? (Concept test)
4. **Synthetic:** What does data say? (Analytics analysis)
5. **Vibe-coded:** Does it feel right? (Gut check + expert review)

## Customer Journey Mapping

### Stages
1. **Awareness:** How do they discover the problem/solution exists?
2. **Consideration:** How do they evaluate options?
3. **Decision:** What makes them choose?
4. **Onboarding:** First experience with the product
5. **Usage:** Ongoing experience
6. **Advocacy:** Do they recommend it?

### For Each Stage
- Actions: What is the customer doing?
- Thoughts: What are they thinking?
- Emotions: How do they feel? (frustrated, confused, delighted)
- Touchpoints: Where do they interact with you?
- Pain points: What's broken or annoying?
- Opportunities: Where can you improve?
