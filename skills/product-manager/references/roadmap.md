# Roadmap Planning

## Now/Next/Later Framework

### Now (This Sprint/Month)
- Committed work with detailed user stories
- Clear acceptance criteria
- Engineering estimates complete
- Dependencies identified and resolved

### Next (1-3 Months)
- Planned epics with rough scope
- High-level user stories
- Preliminary engineering input
- Dependencies identified (not necessarily resolved)

### Later (3-6 Months)
- Directional themes and strategic bets
- Outcome-focused (not feature-focused)
- Subject to change based on learnings
- No date commitments

## Roadmap Communication Rules

1. **Never commit to dates for "Later" items** — they will change
2. **Show outcomes, not features** — "Improve activation to 40%" not "Build onboarding wizard"
3. **Include what you're NOT doing** — and why (this builds trust)
4. **Review and update monthly** — stale roadmaps lose credibility
5. **Different views for different audiences:**
   - Engineering: Detailed, sprint-level, technical
   - Leadership: Strategic, outcome-focused, quarterly
   - Customers: High-level themes, no specific dates
   - Sales: Feature-focused, rough timing, competitive context

## Roadmap Inputs

### Where Roadmap Ideas Come From
1. **Strategy:** Company OKRs and strategic priorities
2. **Customer feedback:** Interviews, support tickets, NPS comments
3. **Data:** Product analytics, funnel metrics, retention analysis
4. **Competitive:** What competitors are building (and what they're not)
5. **Engineering:** Tech debt, infrastructure needs, scalability
6. **Sales:** Deals lost, feature requests from prospects
7. **Support:** Common issues, frequently asked questions

### How to Say No
- "That's a great idea. Right now, [priority X] has higher impact because [reason]."
- "We've added it to the backlog. Currently, it's lower priority than [X, Y, Z] because [data]."
- "Interesting. Can you help me understand the business case? Specifically, how many customers are affected and what's the revenue impact?"
