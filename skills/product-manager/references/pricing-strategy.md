# Pricing Strategy

## Value Metric Selection

The value metric is the unit you charge for. It should:
- Align with how customers get value
- Be easy to understand
- Grow as the customer grows
- Be predictable for the customer

### Common Value Metrics
| Metric | Example | Best For |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Per user/seat | Slack, Notion | Collaboration tools |
| Per usage/volume | Stripe, Twilio | API/infrastructure |
| Per feature tier | Most SaaS | Multi-segment products |
| Per outcome | Performance marketing | Results-based |
| Per storage/data | Dropbox, S3 | Storage products |
| Flat rate | Basecamp | Simple products |

## Pricing Models

### Freemium
- Free tier with limited functionality
- Conversion target: 2-5%
- Best for: Products with network effects, viral potential
- Risk: Free tier too generous (no upgrade motivation)

### Free Trial
- Full access for limited time (7, 14, or 30 days)
- Conversion target: 10-25%
- Best for: Products where value is clear after use
- Risk: Time too short to reach "aha moment"

### Per-Seat Pricing
- Charge per user
- Predictable for buyer
- Risk: Discourages adoption (seat-sharing)

### Usage-Based
- Pay for what you use
- Aligns cost with value
- Harder to predict revenue
- Risk: Revenue volatility

### Hybrid
- Base platform fee + usage/seat charges
- Most common for growth-stage SaaS
- Best revenue predictability

## Tier Design (3-4 Tiers)

### Tier 1: Free/Starter
- Purpose: Reduce friction, let users experience value
- Features: Core functionality, hard limits
- CTA: "Get started free"

### Tier 2: Professional (Most Popular)
- Purpose: Where most customers land
- Features: Full functionality, reasonable limits
- Mark as "Most Popular" (social proof)
- This is your primary revenue tier

### Tier 3: Business/Team
- Purpose: Growing teams with more needs
- Features: Everything in Pro + collaboration, integrations
- Higher limits

### Tier 4: Enterprise
- Purpose: Large organizations
- Features: Everything + SSO, SLA, custom integrations
- "Contact Sales" pricing

## Pricing Psychology

- **Anchoring:** Show the highest tier first
- **Charm pricing:** $49 vs $50 (B2C). Less effective in B2B
- **Value framing:** "$2/day" or "Saves 10 hours/week"
- **Annual discount:** 15-20% off for annual payment
- **Decoy effect:** Add a tier that makes target tier look better
- **Bundling:** Package features to increase perceived value

## Pricing Page Best Practices
- Show all tiers side-by-side
- Highlight recommended tier
- Use toggle for monthly/annual pricing
- Feature comparison table below tiers
- FAQ section for pricing objections
- Social proof (logos, testimonials)
- Money-back guarantee or free trial CTA
