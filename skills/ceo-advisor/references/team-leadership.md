# Team Leadership

A-player hiring, first 10 hires, delegation, and culture building for startup CEOs. Based on research-backed frameworks for building high-performance teams from scratch.

## The First 10 Hires

Your first 10 hires define your company's DNA. They set the culture, standards, and velocity for everything that follows.

### Hiring Priorities by Stage

| Stage | Headcount | Priority Hires |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| **Solo founder** | 0 | Contractor help only |
| **Pre-seed** | 1-3 | Co-founder, first engineer, first sales/growth |
| **Seed** | 3-8 | Engineering lead, designer, first CS, ops |
| **Series A** | 8-20 | VP Eng, VP Sales, PM, marketing, 2-3 more engineers |
| **Series B** | 20-50 | VPs across functions, managers, specialists |

### First 10 Hire Attributes

Every early hire MUST have:
1. **Generalist ability** — Will wear 3 hats minimum
2. **Builder mentality** — Creates from nothing, doesn't need structure
3. **Low ego** — No "that's not my job" attitude
4. **Speed** — Bias for action over perfection
5. **Culture fit (add, not fit)** — Adds something new while sharing core values

### The Hire Sequence Mistake

Most founders hire in this order: Engineer → Designer → Marketer → Sales

**Better order for B2B:** Engineer → Sales/Growth → Designer → Marketer
**Better order for B2C:** Engineer → Designer → Growth → Community

**Why:** Revenue-generating roles should come before polish roles.

## A-Player Hiring Framework

### Identifying A-Players

| Trait | A-Player | B-Player | C-Player |
|-------|----------|----------|----------|
| Output | 10x peers | Expected output | Below expectations |
| Initiative | Creates opportunities | Waits for direction | Avoids responsibility |
| Quality | Sets new standards | Meets standards | Cuts corners |
| Speed | Learns fast, ships fast | Steady pace | Needs hand-holding |
| Impact | Transforms the function | Maintains the function | Drags it down |

### The Interview Process

**Step 1: Resume screen (2 min)**
- Look for trajectory, not pedigree
- Achievements > responsibilities
- Specific metrics > vague descriptions

**Step 2: Phone screen (20 min)**
- Can they articulate what they've built?
- Do they ask smart questions about YOUR business?
- Energy and enthusiasm level?

**Step 3: Work sample or challenge (1-3 hours)**
- Give a real problem from your business
- Evaluate thinking process, not just answer
- Pay for their time (seriously — $100-300 for a quality take-home)

**Step 4: Deep dive interview (60 min)**
- Walk me through [biggest achievement] — who, what, how, results?
- Tell me about a time you failed — what happened, what did you learn?
- What would your last manager say is your biggest weakness?
- What would you do in your first 30/60/90 days here?

**Step 5: Reference checks (ALWAYS)**
- Call 3 references they provide + 1-2 back-channel
- Key question: "Would you hire them again?" (anything other than instant "yes" is a red flag)

### Compensation for Early Hires

| Component | Pre-seed | Seed | Series A |
|-----------|----------|------|----------|
| Cash salary | Below market (60-80%) | Slightly below (80-90%) | Near market |
| Equity | 1-5% for first 5 | 0.25-1% | 0.1-0.5% |
| Vesting | 4 year, 1 year cliff | 4 year, 1 year cliff | 4 year, 1 year cliff |

## Delegation Framework

### The Delegation Ladder

1. **Look into this and give me all the facts** (you decide)
2. **Give me options with pros/cons** (you decide)
3. **Recommend an action; I'll approve** (they think, you approve)
4. **Decide and tell me what you did** (they decide, you're informed)
5. **Decide and act; no need to tell me** (full autonomy)

**Mistake:** Most founders stay at level 1-2 forever. Your job is to move reports UP the ladder as fast as possible.

### What to Delegate First

| Delegate NOW | Keep for Now |
|-------------|-------------|
| Operational tasks | Vision and strategy |
| Customer support | Key customer relationships |
| Routine decisions | Hiring decisions (early stage) |
| Process documentation | Culture and values |
| Analytics and reporting | Fundraising |

### Signs You're Not Delegating Enough
- You're in >10 meetings/day
- Team asks permission for everything
- You're doing work someone else could do at 80% quality
- You haven't had a deep work block in weeks
- You know every detail of every project

## Culture Building

### Culture Is What You Do, Not What You Say

Culture = the behaviors that get rewarded + the behaviors that get punished.

**If you say "we value innovation" but fire someone for a failed experiment, your real culture is risk-aversion.**

### Building Culture Intentionally

**Step 1: Define 3-5 values (specific and actionable)**

| Weak Value | Strong Value |
|-----------|-------------|
| "Excellence" | "Ship on Friday, iterate on Monday" |
| "Integrity" | "Bad news travels fast — escalate within 24 hours" |
| "Teamwork" | "No task is below anyone — founders included" |
| "Innovation" | "Default to yes on experiments under $500" |

**Step 2: Model the values visibly**
- Founders must be the loudest demonstration
- If you say "work-life balance" but email at midnight, nobody believes you
- Your calendar should reflect your stated priorities

**Step 3: Hire for values**
- Ask behavioral questions that test values
- Reject A-players who don't share values (hardest but most important decision)
- Every hire either strengthens or dilutes culture

**Step 4: Enforce the values**
- Address violations immediately, privately, directly
- Public recognition for value-aligned behavior
- Fire fast for values violations (yes, even top performers)

### The Culture Killers

1. **Brilliant jerks** — One toxic A-player destroys 3-5 good people's productivity
2. **Founders above the rules** — "Values are for everyone else"
3. **Unresolved conflict** — Avoiding hard conversations creates passive-aggressive culture
4. **Unclear ownership** — When everyone is responsible, no one is
5. **Information hoarding** — Transparency builds trust; secrecy builds paranoia

## Managing Performance

### The 30/60/90 Day Plan

Every new hire should have:
- **Day 30:** Understand the business, complete onboarding, ship first small win
- **Day 60:** Own their area, identify improvement opportunities, build key relationships
- **Day 90:** Operate independently, deliver meaningful results, propose next quarter's goals

### Feedback Framework

**SBI Model:**
- **Situation:** "In yesterday's client call..."
- **Behavior:** "...you interrupted the client three times..."
- **Impact:** "...which made them feel unheard and they went cold on us."

**Give feedback within 24 hours.** Delayed feedback is useless.

### When to Fire

**Three strikes, but fast:**
1. Clear feedback + specific improvement plan + timeline (2-4 weeks)
2. Second check-in — if no improvement, final warning with exit date
3. If still no improvement → exit with dignity and support

**Exception:** Values violations = immediate exit. One strike.

**The rule:** If you're thinking about firing someone, you're probably 3 months too late.
