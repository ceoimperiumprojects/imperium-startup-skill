# Board Meeting Preparation

## Board Meeting Calendar

| Quarter | Focus Areas |
|---------|-------------|
| Q1 | Annual strategy review, previous year performance, current year priorities |
| Q2 | Q1 results, strategic initiative progress, competitive landscape, talent review |
| Q3 | Mid-year performance, budget preview, strategic planning, succession planning |
| Q4 | Annual budget approval, executive compensation, board evaluation |

## Preparation Timeline

**T-4 Weeks:** Draft agenda, begin pre-read preparation
**T-2 Weeks:** Materials to review, final agenda confirmation
**T-1 Week:** Board package distribution, pre-meeting calls
**T-0:** Executive session → Board meeting → Follow-up actions

## Board Package Template

### 1. Cover Memo (1 page)
- Meeting agenda with time allocations
- Key decisions required
- Strategic questions for the board

### 2. CEO Report (3-5 pages)
- Executive summary (3 bullets max)
- Performance highlights with metrics
- Strategic progress update
- Key challenges and mitigation
- Specific asks from the board

### 3. Financial Report (5-10 pages)
- Financial statements (income, balance sheet, cash flow)
- KPI dashboard with trends
- Budget vs Actual variance analysis
- Cash position and runway
- Updated forecast

### 4. Strategic Updates (10-15 pages)
- Initiative status (Green/Yellow/Red for each)
- Market analysis and trends
- Competitive intelligence
- Product roadmap progress

### 5. Appendices
- Detailed financial data
- Supporting research
- Previous meeting minutes

## Managing Board Dynamics

### Delivering Bad News
1. Don't delay — inform promptly (never let them find out elsewhere)
2. Lead with facts, not emotions
3. Own responsibility
4. Present action plan
5. Set realistic recovery timeline

### Handling Difficult Board Members
- Listen fully before responding
- Acknowledge concerns with specific data
- Offer to take offline for deeper discussion
- Follow up with written summary
- If persistent conflict: involve board chair privately

### Board NPS (Self-Assessment)
Ask yourself quarterly:
- Do board members return calls within 24 hours?
- Do they proactively send useful introductions?
- Are board discussions strategic (not just reporting)?
- Do you look forward to board meetings?
If 3+ are "no" → Your board relationship needs work.
