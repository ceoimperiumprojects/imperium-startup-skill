# Fundraising Playbook

## Fundraising Readiness

### Know Your Numbers Cold
Before any investor meeting, be able to answer without hesitation:
- MRR/ARR and growth rate
- Burn rate and runway (months remaining)
- CAC and LTV (even estimates at early stage)
- Net Revenue Retention
- Number of customers and growth trajectory
- TAM/SAM/SOM (bottom-up, not top-down)

### Materials Checklist
- [ ] Pitch deck (10-12 slides, storytelling arc)
- [ ] Executive summary (1-pager)
- [ ] Financial model (3-year, three scenarios)
- [ ] Cap table (current, clean)
- [ ] Demo (working product or convincing prototype)
- [ ] Data room (organized, professional)

## Fundraising Strategy

### How Much to Raise
**Formula:** Monthly burn × 18-24 months + key milestones budget
- Too little: You run out before next raise
- Too much: Unnecessary dilution and pressure to grow into valuation
- Sweet spot: Enough for 18-24 months with clear milestones

### Instrument Selection

| Instrument | Best For | Pros | Cons |
|-----------|---------|------|------|
| SAFE | Pre-seed, seed | Simple, fast, cheap | No valuation cap negotiation leverage |
| Convertible Note | Seed, bridge | Interest accrual, maturity date creates urgency | Debt on balance sheet |
| Priced Round | Series A+ | Clear valuation, proper governance | Expensive legal, longer process |

### Valuation Benchmarks (2024-2026)

| Stage | Typical Range | Based On |
|-------|---------------|----------|
| Pre-seed | $2-6M | Team, idea, market |
| Seed | $5-15M | Early traction, TAM |
| Series A | $15-50M | Revenue, growth rate, unit economics |
| Series B | $50-200M | Scaling proof, path to profitability |

## Investor Targeting

### Tier Your Investors
- **Tier A (Dream):** Perfect thesis match, strong brand, ideal check size
- **Tier B (Good Fit):** Good match, helpful network, right stage
- **Tier C (Backup):** Acceptable terms, less strategic value

### Research Each Investor
- Recent investments (do they invest in your space?)
- Check size (does your raise match?)
- Stage focus (pre-seed vs growth)
- Portfolio conflicts (do they back a competitor?)
- Decision maker (partner vs associate)
- Warm intro path (who do you know who knows them?)

### Outreach Strategy
1. Start with Tier B investors (practice rounds)
2. Create momentum signals (social proof, urgency)
3. Move to Tier A with confidence and practice
4. Aim for parallel processes (multiple term sheets = leverage)

## The Pitch Meeting

### Structure (30-45 minutes)
1. **Small talk** (2-3 min): Build rapport, find common ground
2. **Your story** (10-12 min): Problem → Solution → Traction → Market → Team → Ask
3. **Demo** (5 min): Show, don't tell
4. **Q&A** (15-20 min): This is where you win or lose
5. **Next steps** (2 min): Clear timeline and action items

### Top 20 Investor Questions (Prepare For All)
1. Why this problem? Why now?
2. How big is the market? (Bottom-up math)
3. Who are you competing against?
4. Why will you win?
5. What's your unfair advantage?
6. How do customers find you?
7. What are your unit economics?
8. What's the business model?
9. Why is this team the right team?
10. What's the biggest risk?
11. How will you use the money?
12. What milestones will this round achieve?
13. What happens if you don't raise?
14. What's your hiring plan?
15. Tell me about your churn
16. What's your path to profitability?
17. How do you think about pricing?
18. What have you learned from customers?
19. What would make you pivot?
20. Who else are you talking to?

## Term Sheet Essentials

### Key Terms to Understand

| Term | Standard | Red Flag |
|------|----------|----------|
| Liquidation preference | 1x non-participating | >1x or participating |
| Board composition | Founder control through Series A | Investor majority at seed |
| Pro-rata rights | Standard for lead investor | Required for all investors |
| Anti-dilution | Broad-based weighted average | Full ratchet |
| Vesting | 4 years, 1-year cliff | Acceleration stripped |
| Option pool | 10-15% (negotiate pre/post-money) | >20% pre-money |
| Information rights | Quarterly financial statements | Excessive reporting burden |

### Negotiation Tips
- Valuation is not the only term — focus on the whole package
- Understand what's market standard (ask your lawyer)
- Having multiple term sheets gives you leverage
- Don't negotiate against yourself
- Get a startup-experienced lawyer (not your uncle's corporate attorney)

## Post-Close

- [ ] Thank all investors (including those who passed — build relationships)
- [ ] Set up monthly investor update cadence
- [ ] Update cap table immediately
- [ ] File required regulatory paperwork
- [ ] Announce (internally first, then publicly)
- [ ] Start executing on the milestones you promised
