# Pitch Deck Creation Guide

## The Narrative Arc

A great pitch deck tells a story. The arc:

1. **Hook** (Problem): Make the investor FEEL the pain
2. **Relief** (Solution): Show the way out
3. **Proof** (Traction): Demonstrate it's working
4. **Scale** (Market): Show the opportunity is massive
5. **Edge** (Competition + Moat): Show why you win
6. **People** (Team): Show you can execute
7. **Path** (Ask): Show the way forward

## Slide-by-Slide Guide

### Slide 1: Title
- Company name
- One-line tagline (pass the "mom test" — anyone should understand)
- Contact info
- *Pro tip: Make the tagline a benefit, not a description*

### Slide 2: Problem
- Specific, measurable pain point
- Who feels it (named persona)
- How they cope today (current alternatives)
- Why now (market timing, trend, technology shift)
- *Pro tip: Use a story or data point to make it visceral*

### Slide 3: Solution
- Product in one sentence
- How it works (3 steps max)
- Key differentiator
- Screenshot or demo mockup
- *Pro tip: Show, don't tell. If possible, demo live*

### Slide 4: Market Size
- TAM (Total Addressable Market)
- SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)
- SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market, Year 1-3)
- Bottom-up math (# customers × ARPU)
- *Pro tip: Investors care about SAM/SOM, not TAM. Show your math*

### Slide 5: Business Model
- Revenue model (subscription, transaction, etc.)
- Pricing tiers
- Unit economics (CAC, LTV, payback)
- Key assumptions
- *Pro tip: LTV:CAC > 3:1 is the magic ratio*

### Slide 6: Traction
- Key metrics (MRR, users, growth rate)
- Milestones achieved
- Notable customers or LOIs
- Growth trajectory chart (up and to the right)
- *Pro tip: Most important slide for early-stage. Show momentum*

### Slide 7: Competition
- 2x2 positioning matrix (choose axes where you're top-right)
- Your unique positioning
- Defensible advantages
- *Pro tip: Never say "no competition." Use a matrix that highlights your strengths*

### Slide 8: Go-to-Market
- Customer acquisition strategy
- Sales motion (self-serve, PLG, sales-led)
- Key channels
- Early playbook that's working
- *Pro tip: Specificity > ambition. "First 100 through X" beats "digital marketing"*

### Slide 9: Team
- Founders with relevant background
- Why THIS team for THIS problem
- Key hires made or planned
- Notable advisors
- *Pro tip: Founder-market fit is critical. Show domain expertise or unique insight*

### Slide 10: Financials
- Revenue projections (3 years)
- Key assumptions behind projections
- Current burn rate and runway
- Path to profitability
- *Pro tip: Be realistic. Investors stress-test assumptions*

### Slide 11: The Ask
- Amount raising
- Use of funds (3-5 categories with percentages)
- Milestones this enables
- Timeline to next round (or profitability)
- *Pro tip: "€200K to achieve X, Y, Z in 12 months" > "€200K for growth"*

## Stage-Specific Adjustments

### Pre-Seed/Seed
- Lead with problem + team (traction may be limited)
- Pre-revenue traction counts: waitlist, interviews, LOIs, pilots
- Financial projections less important than market insight
- Show velocity of learning

### Series A
- Lead with traction + metrics
- Show clear PMF evidence
- Unit economics must work (or show clear path)
- Detailed go-to-market playbook

### Series B+
- Lead with metrics + growth efficiency
- Show scalability and operational leverage
- Market expansion strategy
- Path to profitability

## Design Principles

- One idea per slide
- No walls of text (if you can't say it in 3 bullets, it's too complex)
- Data > words (charts, numbers, metrics)
- Consistent visual style
- Dark text on light background (easier to read in any setting)
- 12+ font size (readability at a distance)
- No more than 12-15 slides (investor attention spans)
