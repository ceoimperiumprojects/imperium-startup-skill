# Decision Frameworks

## The DECIDE Framework

**D** - Define the problem clearly
**E** - Establish criteria for solutions
**C** - Consider alternatives (minimum 3)
**I** - Identify best alternative using weighted scoring
**D** - Develop and implement action plan
**E** - Evaluate and monitor outcomes

## Go/No-Go Decision Criteria

### For Product Decisions
- [ ] Customer demand validated (>70% interest in interviews)
- [ ] Technical feasibility confirmed
- [ ] Positive unit economics (at least in model)
- [ ] Strategic alignment with company direction
- [ ] Resources available (or can be freed)

### For Market Entry
- [ ] Market size justifies investment
- [ ] Competitive landscape allows entry
- [ ] Regulatory environment manageable
- [ ] Distribution path identified
- [ ] Team has relevant expertise (or can hire)

### For Partnerships
- [ ] Strategic value clear (not just "nice to have")
- [ ] Partner reliable and aligned
- [ ] Revenue impact modeled
- [ ] Exit clause if it doesn't work
- [ ] Resource cost acceptable

## Reversibility Framework

**Type 1 decisions (irreversible):** Take your time. Gather data. Debate.
- Examples: Fundraising, major pivots, key hires, market entry

**Type 2 decisions (reversible):** Move fast. Default to action.
- Examples: Feature priorities, marketing channels, tool selection, pricing tests

**Rule:** Most decisions are Type 2. Don't treat them like Type 1.

## Pre-Mortem Analysis

Before making a major decision, imagine it's 6 months later and the decision failed:
1. What went wrong?
2. What did we miss?
3. What assumptions were wrong?
4. What signals did we ignore?
5. What could we have done differently?

Use answers to stress-test the decision and add safeguards.

## Speed vs Quality Tradeoff

| Decision Impact | Reversibility | Approach |
|----------------|---------------|----------|
| High | Irreversible | Deliberate (days-weeks) |
| High | Reversible | Thoughtful (hours-days) |
| Low | Irreversible | Careful (hours) |
| Low | Reversible | Instant (decide now) |
