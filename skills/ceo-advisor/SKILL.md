# CEO Advisor

Strategic leadership frameworks for vision, fundraising, board management, culture, and stakeholder alignment. Built for startup CEOs navigating from pre-seed through Series B+.

## Keywords

CEO, chief executive officer, strategy, strategic planning, fundraising, board management, investor relations, culture, organizational leadership, vision, mission, stakeholder management, capital allocation, crisis management, succession planning, pitch deck, board deck, annual plan, OKRs, pivot, company direction

## Core Responsibilities

### 1. Vision & Strategy

Set the direction — a clear, compelling answer to "Where are we going and why?"

**Strategic planning cycle:**
- Annual: 3-year vision refresh + 1-year strategic plan
- Quarterly: OKR setting with leadership team
- Monthly: Strategy health check — are we still on track?

**Stage-adaptive time horizons:**
| Stage | Short-term | Medium-term | Long-term |
|-------|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Pre-seed/Seed | 3 months | 6 months | 12 months |
| Series A | 6 months | 1 year | 2 years |
| Series B+ | 1 year | 3 years | 5 years |

**Strategic decision framework (DECIDE):**
- **D**efine the problem clearly
- **E**stablish criteria for solutions
- **C**onsider alternatives
- **I**dentify best alternatives
- **D**evelop and implement action plan
- **E**valuate and monitor solution

See `references/strategy-advisor.md` for full strategic analysis frameworks.

### 2. Capital & Resource Management

You're the chief allocator. Every dollar, person, and engineering hour is a bet.

**Capital allocation priorities:**
1. **Essential (40-50%)** — Operations, compliance, security
2. **Strategic (30-40%)** — Growth initiatives, competitive advantage
3. **Efficiency (10-15%)** — Cost reduction, productivity
4. **Experimental (5-10%)** — Innovation, R&D, new bets

**Budget decision tree:**
- Required for operations? → Auto-approve
- Drives growth with >30% ROI? → Strategic approve
- Reduces costs with <12 month payback? → Efficiency approve
- Everything else? → Limited experimental budget

### 3. Fundraising

Know your numbers cold. Timing matters more than valuation.

**Key fundraising metrics to know:**
- MRR/ARR and growth rate
- Burn rate and runway (months)
- CAC, LTV, and LTV:CAC ratio
- Net Revenue Retention
- TAM/SAM/SOM (bottom-up)

**Process:** See `references/fundraising.md` for full fundraising playbook.
**Materials:** Use `/imperium:pitch-deck` and `/imperium:fundraise-prep` commands.

### 4. Board & Investor Management

Your board can be your greatest asset or your biggest liability.

**Board meeting cadence:**
- Quarterly board meetings (materials sent 3 days ahead)
- Monthly investor updates (see `references/board-prep.md`)
- Ad hoc calls for major decisions

**Board deck structure:** See `references/board-prep.md` for the full board meeting prep framework.

### 5. Organizational Culture

Culture is what people do when you're not in the room.

**Culture development:**
- Define values (3-5, specific and actionable)
- Model them personally (visible and consistent)
- Hire for them (culture add, not culture fit)
- Enforce them (address violations immediately)
- Celebrate them (recognize people who embody values)

### 6. Crisis Management

**Immediate (0-2 hours):** Activate crisis team, assess severity, contain, notify stakeholders
**Short-term (2-24 hours):** Response strategy, public statements, legal/regulatory, employee communication
**Recovery (24+ hours):** Implement solution, full stakeholder communication, post-mortem

## Key Questions a CEO Should Ask

- "Can every person explain our strategy in one sentence?"
- "What's the one thing that, if it goes wrong, kills us?"
- "Am I spending time on the highest-leverage activity right now?"
- "What decision am I avoiding? Why?"
- "If we could only do one thing this quarter, what would it be?"
- "Do investors and team hear the same story from me?"

## Red Flags

- You're the bottleneck for >3 decisions per week
- Burn multiple rising while growth slows
- Board surprises (they should never learn bad news in the meeting)
- Strategic plan exists but nobody references it
- Key hires leaving in first 6 months
