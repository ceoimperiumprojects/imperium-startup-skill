# Marketing

Full-stack startup marketing: positioning, content, SEO, CRO, copywriting, email, social, paid, and growth. Built for startups that need to do more with less.

## Core Domains

### 1. Positioning & Brand

**Geoffrey Moore Positioning Statement:**
For [target customer] who [need/pain point], [product] is a [category] that [key benefit]. Unlike [alternatives], we [key differentiator].

**Brand voice development:**
- Define 3-5 brand attributes (e.g., confident but not arrogant, helpful but not patronizing)
- Create a "we say / we don't say" list
- Document voice guidelines with examples
- Apply consistently across all channels

**Messaging hierarchy:**
1. One-liner (elevator pitch)
2. Value proposition (what + for whom + key benefit)
3. Key messages by persona (3 per persona)
4. Supporting proof points per message

### 2. Content Strategy & SEO

**Content strategy framework:**
- **Goals:** What business outcome? (Traffic → Leads → Revenue)
- **Audience:** Who are we writing for? (Search intent mapping)
- **Topics:** Keyword clusters aligned with buyer journey
- **Calendar:** Publishing cadence (minimum 2-4 posts/month)
- **Distribution:** Where and how content reaches the audience

**SEO fundamentals:**
- Keyword research: volume × intent × difficulty
- On-page: Title tags, H1s, meta descriptions, internal links
- Technical: Core Web Vitals, sitemap, schema markup
- Content: Match search intent, be comprehensive, update regularly
- Off-page: Link building through content quality and outreach

**Content types by funnel stage:**

| Stage | Content Type | Goal |
|-------|-------------|------|
| Top | Blog posts, guides, tools | Awareness, traffic |
| Middle | Case studies, comparisons, webinars | Consideration |
| Bottom | Demo, free trial, pricing page | Conversion |
| Retention | Onboarding emails, help docs, community | Activation, retention |

**Programmatic SEO:** Template-driven pages at scale (e.g., "[Tool] vs [Competitor]", "[Tool] for [Industry]"). High volume, low effort per page.

### 3. Copywriting

**Core principles:**
- Clarity over cleverness
- Benefits over features
- Specificity over vagueness ("Cut reporting from 4 hours to 15 minutes" not "Save time")
- Customer language over company language
- One idea per section
- Active voice, simple words, confident tone

**Page structure (any landing/product page):**
1. **Hero:** Headline + subheadline + CTA + visual
2. **Problem:** Agitate the pain
3. **Solution:** Show the way out
4. **How it works:** 3 steps max
5. **Social proof:** Testimonials, logos, numbers
6. **Features/Benefits:** What they get
7. **Objection handling:** Address concerns
8. **Final CTA:** Repeat the ask

**Headline formulas:**
- [Action verb] + [Desired outcome] + [Timeframe/Qualifier]
- "Stop [pain]. Start [benefit]."
- [Number] + [Noun] + [Promise]
- "The [adjective] way to [desired outcome]"

### 4. Email Marketing

**Key sequence types:**

**Welcome sequence (5-7 emails):**
1. Welcome + quick win (Day 0)
2. Key feature highlight (Day 1)
3. Success story / case study (Day 3)
4. Tips + value content (Day 5)
5. Soft upgrade nudge (Day 7)

**Nurture sequence:**
- Educational content building expertise and trust
- Mix of value emails (80%) and promotional (20%)
- Segment by behavior and engagement level

**Re-engagement sequence:**
- "We miss you" (Day 30 inactive)
- "Here's what's new" (Day 37)
- "Last chance" with incentive (Day 44)
- Unsubscribe or downgrade (Day 51)

**Email best practices:**
- Subject lines: Short, specific, curiosity-driven
- Preview text: Extends the subject line
- One CTA per email
- Mobile-first design
- Send time: Test, but Tuesday-Thursday mornings often win
- Measure: Open rate, click rate, conversion rate, unsubscribe rate

### 5. CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization)

**Optimization framework:**
1. **Measure:** Set up analytics, identify drop-off points
2. **Hypothesize:** "If we [change], then [metric] will [improve] because [reason]"
3. **Test:** A/B test with statistical significance
4. **Learn:** Document results, apply learnings
5. **Repeat:** Continuous improvement cycle

**High-impact CRO targets:**
- Signup flow (reduce fields, remove friction)
- Pricing page (clarity, anchoring, social proof)
- Onboarding (time to first value)
- Landing pages (message match, CTA clarity)
- Forms (progressive disclosure, smart defaults)

**Quick wins:**
- Remove unnecessary form fields
- Add social proof near CTAs
- Make CTAs action-oriented ("Start free trial" not "Submit")
- Reduce page load time
- Add urgency/scarcity (only if genuine)

### 6. Growth & Viral Loops

**Growth loops:**
- **Viral:** User invites → New user → User invites (referral programs)
- **Content:** Create content → SEO traffic → Signups → Create content
- **Paid:** Revenue → Reinvest in ads → New customers → Revenue
- **Sales:** Customer success → Case study → New leads → Sales

**Referral program design:**
- Two-sided incentive (giver and receiver both benefit)
- Simple mechanics (one click to share)
- Integrated into product experience (not bolt-on)
- Track: Viral coefficient (K-factor), referral conversion rate

### 7. Paid Acquisition

**Channel selection:**
- B2B: LinkedIn Ads, Google Search, retargeting
- B2C: Meta (Facebook/Instagram), Google, TikTok
- Both: Google Search (high intent), retargeting (warm audiences)

**Budget allocation (early stage):**
- Start with $500-2000/month per channel
- Test 2-3 channels for 30 days each
- Double down on best-performing, cut the rest
- Target CAC payback within 3-6 months

### 8. Social Media

**Platform priorities by business type:**
- B2B SaaS: LinkedIn > Twitter/X > YouTube
- B2C App: Instagram > TikTok > Twitter/X
- Developer tools: Twitter/X > GitHub > YouTube > Reddit

**Content mix (4:1:1 rule):**
- 4 value/educational posts
- 1 soft promotion
- 1 community/engagement post

## Anti-Patterns

- Building brand before distribution (nobody sees your beautiful brand)
- Creating content without distribution plan
- Optimizing for vanity metrics (followers, likes) vs. revenue metrics
- Trying every channel at once instead of mastering one
- Writing for search engines instead of humans
- A/B testing without statistical significance
- Ignoring customer feedback loops — marketing without talking to real users leads to messaging that sounds good but doesn't convert
