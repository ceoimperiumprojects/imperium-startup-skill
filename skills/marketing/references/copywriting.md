# Copywriting Reference

## Core Principles
1. **Clarity over cleverness** — If choosing between clear and creative, choose clear
2. **Benefits over features** — "Save 4 hours/week" not "Automated reporting engine"
3. **Specificity over vagueness** — Numbers, names, timeframes
4. **Customer language** — Mirror their words from reviews, interviews, support tickets
5. **One idea per section** — Each block advances one argument
6. **Active voice** — "We generate reports" not "Reports are generated"
7. **Honest** — No fabricated stats or testimonials

## Page Structure (Landing/Product Page)

1. **Hero Section**
   - Headline: Clear benefit (what they get)
   - Subheadline: How it works (1 sentence)
   - CTA: Action-oriented button
   - Visual: Product screenshot or hero image

2. **Problem Section**
   - Agitate the pain (make them feel it)
   - Use their language

3. **Solution Section**
   - Show the way out
   - 3 steps max ("How it works")

4. **Social Proof**
   - Customer logos
   - Testimonials with specific results
   - Numbers ("10,000+ teams trust us")

5. **Features/Benefits**
   - Lead with benefit, support with feature
   - Format: Benefit headline + Feature explanation + Visual

6. **Objection Handling**
   - FAQ section
   - Address top 3-5 concerns

7. **Final CTA**
   - Repeat the offer
   - Add urgency if genuine
   - Risk reversal (free trial, money-back guarantee)

## Headline Formulas

| Formula | Example |
|---------|---------|
| [Action] + [Outcome] + [Timeframe] | "Build landing pages in minutes, not weeks" |
| "Stop [pain]. Start [benefit]." | "Stop guessing. Start converting." |
| [Number] + [Noun] + [Promise] | "3 steps to double your reply rate" |
| "The [adjective] way to [outcome]" | "The fastest way to validate your startup idea" |
| "[Outcome] without [pain]" | "Grow revenue without hiring more salespeople" |
| "Why [audience] [action]" | "Why 10,000 founders use Imperium" |

## Writing Style Rules
- "Use" not "utilize"
- "Help" not "facilitate"
- "Start" not "commence"
- Remove: "almost," "very," "really," "just," "actually"
- No exclamation points (they weaken your message)
- No buzzwords: "leverage," "synergy," "innovative," "cutting-edge"
- Read it aloud — if it sounds weird, rewrite it

## CTA Best Practices
- Action verb + outcome: "Start free trial," "Get your report," "Book a demo"
- Avoid: "Submit," "Click here," "Learn more" (weak, generic)
- Button color: Contrasting with page background
- Placement: Above fold + end of every section + bottom of page
- Only ONE primary CTA per page (secondary CTAs can exist but be visually muted)
