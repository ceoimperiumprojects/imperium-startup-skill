# Traction Channels

The Bullseye Framework and 19 traction channels for startups. Distilled from Gabriel Weinberg & Justin Mares' "Traction" (163 chunks in knowledge base).

## The 50% Rule

Spend 50% of your time on product and 50% on traction. Most founders spend 100% on product and wonder why nobody comes. Traction and product development are equally important.

**"Almost every failed startup has a product. What failed startups don't have is traction."**

## The Bullseye Framework

Systematic method to find YOUR best traction channel:

### Outer Ring — Brainstorm (All 19 Channels)
For each of the 19 channels, brainstorm at least one strategy. Don't dismiss anything yet.

### Middle Ring — Test (Top 3-5)
Run cheap tests ($500 and 1 week each) on your top candidates. Measure cost per acquisition and scalability.

### Inner Ring — Focus (The 1)
Double down on the ONE channel that works best. Master it before adding a second.

**Key insight:** The best channel is usually NOT the one you think. Test, don't guess.

## The 19 Traction Channels

### 1. Viral Marketing
- **What:** Users naturally bring in other users through product usage
- **Best for:** Consumer apps, social products, tools with network effects
- **Metric:** Viral coefficient (K-factor) — if >1, exponential growth
- **Example:** Dropbox referral program (both sides get free space)

### 2. Public Relations (PR)
- **What:** Getting covered by media outlets
- **Best for:** Consumer products, fundraising rounds, launches
- **How:** Craft a compelling narrative, target specific reporters, HARO
- **Tip:** Small niche publications often drive more customers than TechCrunch

### 3. Unconventional PR
- **What:** Publicity stunts, viral campaigns, guerrilla marketing
- **Best for:** Consumer brands, local businesses, creative products
- **Example:** DollarShaveClub.com launch video (12M views, $4.7M in orders)

### 4. Search Engine Marketing (SEM)
- **What:** Paid ads on Google/Bing for search queries
- **Best for:** Products solving problems people actively search for
- **Economics:** Start at $500/mo, target long-tail keywords, optimize for CPA
- **Key:** Only works if people search for what you solve

### 5. Social & Display Ads
- **What:** Paid ads on Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter
- **Best for:** B2C (Meta), B2B (LinkedIn), awareness campaigns
- **Start:** $1K/mo per platform, test 3-5 creative variations

### 6. Offline Ads
- **What:** Billboards, radio, TV, print, transit ads
- **Best for:** Local businesses, broad consumer products, brand building
- **Usually NOT for early-stage startups** unless hyper-local

### 7. Search Engine Optimization (SEO)
- **What:** Organic search traffic through content and technical optimization
- **Best for:** Long-term growth, content businesses, SaaS
- **Timeline:** 3-6 months for initial results, 12+ months for significant traffic
- **Key:** Match search intent, be comprehensive, build authority

### 8. Content Marketing
- **What:** Blog posts, guides, videos, podcasts that attract your audience
- **Best for:** B2B SaaS, developer tools, education products
- **Rule:** 80% education, 20% promotion. Be genuinely useful.
- **Distribution matters more than creation**

### 9. Email Marketing
- **What:** Building an email list and nurturing leads
- **Best for:** Nearly everything. Email consistently outperforms social for conversions.
- **Key metrics:** Open rate (20-30%), click rate (2-5%), conversion
- **Start:** Collect emails from day 1, even pre-launch

### 10. Engineering as Marketing
- **What:** Building free tools that attract your target audience
- **Best for:** Technical products, SaaS, developer tools
- **Examples:** HubSpot Website Grader, Moz SEO tools, Stripe Atlas guides
- **Key:** Tool must be genuinely useful, not just a thinly veiled sales pitch

### 11. Targeting Blogs
- **What:** Getting featured on blogs your audience already reads
- **Best for:** Niche products, B2B, developer tools
- **How:** Guest posts, sponsorships, product reviews, podcast appearances
- **Tip:** A blog with 10K readers in your niche > a blog with 1M general readers

### 12. Business Development (BD)
- **What:** Strategic partnerships with complementary companies
- **Best for:** B2B, platforms, marketplace businesses
- **Types:** Integration partnerships, co-marketing, distribution deals, reseller agreements
- **Key:** Win-win. Both sides must benefit clearly.

### 13. Sales
- **What:** Direct, 1-on-1 selling to prospects
- **Best for:** B2B, high-ticket, complex products
- **When to start:** When you understand the customer well enough to predict objections
- **Founders should sell first** — before hiring salespeople

### 14. Affiliate Programs
- **What:** Paying others a commission for sending you customers
- **Best for:** Consumer products, SaaS with clear value prop
- **Typical commission:** 20-40% of first payment or $50-200 per customer
- **Key:** Recruit affiliates who already have your audience's trust

### 15. Existing Platforms
- **What:** Building on platforms where your users already are
- **Best for:** Apps, extensions, integrations, marketplace sellers
- **Examples:** Shopify App Store, Chrome Extensions, Salesforce AppExchange
- **Key:** Be where the users already are, don't make them come to you

### 16. Trade Shows
- **What:** Industry conferences and exhibitions
- **Best for:** B2B, enterprise, niche industries
- **ROI tip:** Don't just attend — speak, host a booth, throw a dinner
- **Budget:** $2K-10K per show (booth, travel, materials)

### 17. Offline Events
- **What:** Meetups, workshops, dinners you organize
- **Best for:** Local businesses, community building, high-touch B2B
- **Start small:** 10-person dinner > 200-person conference
- **Key:** Provide genuine value, not just networking

### 18. Speaking Engagements
- **What:** Presenting at conferences, podcasts, webinars
- **Best for:** Thought leadership, B2B, consulting, SaaS
- **Start:** Local meetups and podcasts, then level up
- **Tip:** Record everything — repurpose into content

### 19. Community Building
- **What:** Creating a community around your product/mission
- **Best for:** Developer tools, lifestyle brands, B2B SaaS
- **Platforms:** Discord, Slack, Circle, Reddit, in-product forums
- **Key:** Community must provide value independent of your product

## Channel Selection Cheat Sheet

| Startup Type | Top 3 Channels to Test |
|-------------|------------------------|
| B2B SaaS | Sales, Content/SEO, SEM |
| B2C App | Viral, Social Ads, Content |
| Marketplace | Existing Platforms, SEO, Community |
| Dev Tools | Community, Content, Engineering as Marketing |
| Local Business | Offline Events, SEO, BD |
| E-commerce | Social Ads, SEO, Affiliate |
| Enterprise | Sales, Trade Shows, BD |

```
Budget:       $500 max
Timeline:     1 week
Test:         [specific action]
Success:      [specific metric threshold]
Result:       [what happened]
Decision:     Scale / Iterate / Kill
```
