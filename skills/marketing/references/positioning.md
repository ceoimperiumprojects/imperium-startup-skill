# Brand Positioning & Messaging

## Geoffrey Moore Positioning Statement
For [target customer] who [need/pain point], [product] is a [category] that [key benefit]. Unlike [alternatives], we [key differentiator].

## Messaging Hierarchy

### Level 1: One-Liner (Elevator Pitch)
- 10 words or less
- Anyone should understand it
- Example: "AI vocal coach that helps you sing better, faster"

### Level 2: Value Proposition
- What you do + for whom + key benefit
- Example: "Tunia uses AI to give amateur singers personalized vocal coaching that adapts to their voice in real-time"

### Level 3: Key Messages by Persona (3 per persona)
For each persona, 3 messages that address their specific pain/gain:
- Message 1: Pain-focused (what they struggle with)
- Message 2: Gain-focused (what they achieve)
- Message 3: Differentiator (why you, not alternatives)

### Level 4: Proof Points
Evidence supporting each message:
- Customer quotes
- Data/metrics
- Case studies
- Awards/recognition
- Third-party validation

## Brand Voice Development

### Define 3-5 Brand Attributes
Example spectrum:
- Confident ←→ Arrogant
- Helpful ←→ Patronizing
- Direct ←→ Blunt
- Playful ←→ Unprofessional

### Create "We Say / We Don't Say" List
| We Say | We Don't Say |
|--------|-------------|
| "Help you" | "Leverage synergies" |
| "Simple" | "Cutting-edge solution" |
| "Results" | "Paradigm shift" |

### Voice Consistency Checklist
- [ ] Would a human say this in conversation?
- [ ] Is it specific (not generic)?
- [ ] Does it avoid jargon?
- [ ] Would a 15-year-old understand it?
- [ ] Does it sound like US (not any company)?
