# Entity Structure Guide

## Entity Comparison

| Factor | LLC | C-Corp (Delaware) | S-Corp |
|--------|-----|-------------------|--------|
| VC funding | Difficult | Standard | Not compatible |
| Taxation | Pass-through | Double taxation | Pass-through |
| Equity | Complex units | Simple stock | Limited shareholders |
| Complexity | Low | Medium | Medium |
| Best for | Lifestyle, consulting | VC-backed startup | Small profitable business |

## Delaware C-Corp (Default for Startups)

### Why Delaware?
- Most VCs require it
- Well-established corporate law
- Chancery Court (business-specialized)
- Standard templates (NVCA) assume Delaware
- No state tax for companies with no Delaware operations

### Setup Checklist
- [ ] File Certificate of Incorporation with Delaware
- [ ] Obtain EIN from IRS
- [ ] Create bylaws
- [ ] Hold initial board meeting (minutes)
- [ ] Issue founder shares (at par value, with vesting)
- [ ] File 83(b) election within 30 days of share purchase
- [ ] Qualify to do business in your operating state
- [ ] Set up registered agent in Delaware

### 83(b) Election — CRITICAL
- File within 30 DAYS of receiving shares
- Saves potentially massive tax liability
- There is no extension or exception
- Mail to IRS with certified mail, keep receipt
- Most expensive mistake founders make if missed

## International Considerations

### US Entity with Foreign Founders
- Still recommended: Delaware C-Corp
- Need ITIN or EIN for tax purposes
- May need US bank account (Mercury, Relay)
- Consider tax treaty implications
- Consult with international tax attorney

### EU Entity Considerations
- Consider country of primary operations
- VAT registration requirements
- GDPR compliance implications
- Employment law differences
- EU trademark registration (EUIPO)
