### Customer Pains
What annoys, frustrates, or blocks them?

- **Undesired outcomes:** What results do they want to avoid?
- **Obstacles:** What prevents them from getting the job done?
- **Risks:** What could go wrong?

**Rate each pain:** Mild → Extreme

### Customer Gains
What outcomes and benefits do they want?

- **Required gains:** Basic expectations (without these, solution doesn't work)
- **Expected gains:** Standard expectations (table stakes)
- **Desired gains:** Things they'd love but don't expect
- **Unexpected gains:** Things they don't know they want yet

**Rate each gain:** Nice-to-have → Essential

## Fit Assessment

### Problem-Solution Fit
- [ ] You've identified jobs that matter to customers
- [ ] You've confirmed pains are real (not assumed)
- [ ] Your solution addresses the most important jobs/pains/gains
- [ ] Evidence: Customer interviews, surveys, or behavioral data

### Product-Market Fit Indicators
- [ ] Customers are actively seeking solutions (pull, not push)
- [ ] Customers express willingness to pay
- [ ] Retention metrics show ongoing value
- [ ] Word-of-mouth referrals happening organically

## How to Use

1. **Start with Customer Profile** — always start from the customer side
2. **Use evidence, not assumptions** — base this on real interviews (see Mom Test)
3. **Focus on top 3** — prioritize the most important jobs, worst pains, biggest gains
4. **Check fit** — draw lines connecting your value map to customer profile
5. **Iterate** — update as you learn more from customers
