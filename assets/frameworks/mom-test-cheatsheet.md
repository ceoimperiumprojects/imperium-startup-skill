# The Mom Test — Cheatsheet

Based on Rob Fitzpatrick's "The Mom Test" methodology for customer interviews.

## Core Rules

### Rule 1: Talk about their life, not your idea
- ❌ "Would you use a product that does X?"
- ✅ "How do you currently handle X?"
- ✅ "What's the hardest part about doing X?"

### Rule 2: Ask about specifics in the past, not generics or opinions about the future
- ❌ "Would you ever...?"
- ❌ "How much would you pay for...?"
- ✅ "When's the last time you dealt with X?"
- ✅ "How much did that cost you last time?"
- ✅ "Walk me through what happened..."

### Rule 3: Talk less, listen more
- Target: You talk 20%, they talk 80%
- Ask follow-up questions, not leading questions
- Silence is your friend — let them fill gaps

## Great Questions

### Understanding the Problem
- "What's the hardest part about [doing this thing]?"
- "Tell me about the last time that happened."
- "Why was that hard?"
- "What have you tried to solve it?"
- "What don't you love about the solutions you've tried?"

### Gauging Importance
- "How often does this come up?"
- "How are you dealing with it now?"
- "How much does this cost you (time/money/stress)?"
- "Where does this rank in your priorities?"

### Testing Willingness to Pay
- "Is there a budget for solving this?"
- "How much are you paying for [current solution]?"
- "Who else is involved in this decision?"
- "What would it take to switch from your current approach?"

### Getting Commitments
- "Can I follow up with you when we have a prototype?"
- "Would you be willing to try a beta?"
- "Who else should I talk to about this?"
- "Can you introduce me to [relevant person]?"

## Red Flags in Interviews

| Red Flag | What They Say | What They Mean |
|----------|--------------|----------------|
| **Compliments** | "That's a great idea!" | They're being polite |
| **Fluff** | "I would definitely use that" | Future tense = worthless |
| **Ideas** | "You should also add..." | They're brainstorming, not buying |
| **Generic claims** | "Everyone has this problem" | They're not speaking from experience |

## Green Flags in Interviews

| Green Flag | Signal |
|------------|--------|
| **Specifics** | They describe exact situations, dates, numbers |
| **Emotion** | They get frustrated/excited talking about the problem |
| **Current spend** | They're already paying to solve this somehow |
| **Active search** | They've looked for solutions recently |
| **Commitment** | They offer time, money, reputation, or introductions |

## Interview Structure (15-20 min)

1. **Context** (2 min): "Tell me about your role / how you handle X"
2. **Problem exploration** (10 min): Past behavior, pain points, current solutions
3. **Wrapping up** (3 min): Commitments, introductions, follow-up
4. **Notes** (5 min after): Write up key facts within 5 minutes

## Batch Size

- **Rule of thumb:** 5 conversations to spot patterns, 10 to be confident
- **Stop when you hear the same things** repeatedly
- **Talk to different segments** — patterns within segments, not across
