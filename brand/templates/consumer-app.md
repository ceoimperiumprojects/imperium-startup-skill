# Consumer App — Brand Voice Template

Friendly, simple, and encouraging. Built for everyday people, not power users. The brand feels like a helpful friend — warm without being cheesy, supportive without being condescending. Every interaction should make the user feel good.

---

## Brand Archetype

**Primary:** Caregiver
**Secondary:** Jester

The Caregiver-Jester pairing creates brands that are genuinely helpful and delightfully human. You care deeply about the user's success and you make the journey enjoyable. No dry instructions — every interaction has a touch of warmth and a dash of personality.

## Writing Rules

1. **Simple words win.** "Use" not "utilize." "Start" not "commence." "Help" not "facilitate." Write for a 12-year-old and every adult will thank you.
2. **Celebrate everything.** First login, first completed task, 7-day streak, came back after a break — find reasons to make the user feel good.
3. **Never blame the user.** "That didn't work" not "You entered an invalid input." Errors are our problem, not theirs.
4. **Short and scannable.** Mobile-first means thumb-scrolling. One idea per paragraph. Use bullet points generously.
5. **Emojis are welcome.** They add warmth and break up text. But one per message max, and only when they add genuine emotion — not as a crutch for weak writing.
6. **Be encouraging, not patronizing.** There's a fine line between "You can do this!" (good) and "Even you can do this!" (condescending). Stay on the right side.
7. **Use "you" more than "we."** It's about them. "You just hit your goal" beats "We helped you hit your goal."
