# Tech Startup — Brand Voice Template

A modern, confident tech startup that respects its users' intelligence. Slightly irreverent, always clear, never corporate. Thinks of itself as the scrappy underdog building the future.

---

## Brand Archetype

**Primary:** Innovator
**Secondary:** Rebel

The Innovator-Rebel combo is the classic disruptor brand. You see what's broken in the industry and you're building the fix — not because it's trendy, but because you can't stand the status quo. Your brand radiates forward momentum and quiet defiance of "how things have always been done."

## Sample Copy

### Tagline
> Build what matters. Ship what works.

### Landing Page Hero
> Your infrastructure shouldn't be the thing that slows you down. We built a deployment platform that gets out of your way — zero config, instant rollbacks, and logs that actually tell you what went wrong. Start shipping in 5 minutes, not 5 sprints.

### Email Subject
> Your deploys just got 10x faster (no, really)

### Tweet
> Hot take: If your deploy process has a "please don't touch anything" Slack message, you don't have a deploy process. You have a prayer. We fixed that. Link in bio.

### Error Message
> That didn't work — but we caught it. Here's what happened: [error detail]. Try again, or check our status page if this keeps happening.

## Writing Rules

1. **Lead with the outcome, not the feature.** "Deploy in 5 minutes" not "We offer a deployment tool."
2. **Use specific numbers.** "73% faster" beats "significantly faster" every time.
3. **Contractions always.** "We're" not "We are." "Don't" not "Do not." We're humans talking to humans.
4. **Short paragraphs.** Three lines max. White space is a feature.
5. **No corporate filler.** Delete "leverage," "synergy," "innovative solution," and "at the end of the day."
6. **Technical when it matters.** Don't dumb it down, but don't gatekeep either. Make complex ideas accessible without being patronizing.
7. **Have a point of view.** Opinions are a feature. "We think X is wrong and here's why" is better than "Some people prefer X while others prefer Y."
