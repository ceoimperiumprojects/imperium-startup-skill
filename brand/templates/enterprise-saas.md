## Writing Rules

1. **Substance over sizzle.** Every claim needs evidence — a number, a case study, a framework. Never make an empty promise.
2. **Respect the reader's time.** Get to the point. Executive audiences scan — lead with the insight, follow with the detail.
3. **Avoid superlatives.** "Best-in-class" and "world-leading" are meaningless. "Trusted by 200+ enterprises" is concrete.
4. **Use "we" strategically.** "We" for commitments and actions. "Our platform" for product references. Never "I."
5. **Industry terminology is expected.** Your audience knows what SOC 2, GDPR, and SLA mean. Don't over-explain, but also don't assume knowledge of your proprietary terms.
6. **Measured confidence.** State facts with conviction. Express opinions with supporting evidence. Never hedge unnecessarily ("We believe we might be able to...").
7. **Professional warmth.** Authoritative doesn't mean cold. Acknowledge the human behind the decision. "We understand the pressure of audit season" goes a long way.
